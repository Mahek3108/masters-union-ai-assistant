Bharat Fellowship – Immersion-Driven Business Programme

Bharat Fellowship is a short-term experiential programme designed for young learners aged 18–21 who want to understand how real businesses operate across India. Instead of learning only through classrooms, participants travel across multiple cities, visiting markets, factories, startups, and institutions where business decisions happen daily.

The programme combines travel, industry immersion, CXO interactions, and hands-on business challenges to help students understand India’s diverse economic ecosystem.

Learning Journey Across India

Participants travel through multiple business hubs across India. Each hub provides exposure to a different industry, economic environment, and cultural ecosystem.

Hub 1 – Delhi NCR

The journey begins in Delhi, where participants explore India’s political, commercial, and startup ecosystems.

Key learning experiences include:

Exploring Delhi’s traditional retail markets

Understanding airport cargo logistics and supply chains

Visiting Parliament to observe governance and policy systems

Exposure to Makers’ Labs and innovation environments

Studying luxury brand positioning through Mercedes-Benz dealership experiences

Attending sessions and insights from industry CXOs

This hub introduces participants to how policy, trade, logistics, and consumer markets intersect in India’s capital.

Hub 2 – Dehradun

Dehradun provides exposure to emerging entrepreneurship, education ecosystems, and regional economic development.

Participants gain insights into:

Local enterprise development

Sustainability-driven businesses

Regional supply chains and tourism economies

Hub 3 – Jalandhar

Jalandhar represents India’s manufacturing and export-driven economy.

Participants explore:

Small and medium manufacturing industries

Global export supply chains

Product manufacturing for international markets

Hub 4 – Jaipur

Jaipur offers exposure to heritage businesses and modern entrepreneurship.

Participants learn about:

Tourism-driven business ecosystems

Handicraft and artisan industries

Luxury and heritage brand positioning

Hub 5 – Mumbai

Mumbai introduces participants to India’s corporate and financial ecosystem.

Key learning areas include:

Corporate leadership and large enterprises

Financial markets and investment ecosystems

Media, entertainment, and startup industries

Hub 6 – Bengaluru

Bengaluru represents India’s technology and startup hub.

Participants explore:

Startup ecosystems and venture funding

Technology product development

Innovation and digital business models

Learning Through Immersions

The fellowship includes 25+ immersive experiences where students interact directly with companies, entrepreneurs, institutions, and cultural communities.

These immersions allow participants to observe:

Factory operations and manufacturing processes

Market dynamics and consumer behaviour

Startup innovation and entrepreneurship

Logistics networks and supply chains

Cultural ecosystems that shape regional economies

Hands-On Business Challenges

Alongside travel immersions, participants work on practical business challenges that encourage real-world problem solving.

These challenges involve:

Analysing real businesses and identifying growth opportunities

Developing solutions for operational or market challenges

Presenting insights derived from field observations

This approach helps students apply theoretical ideas to practical situations.

Learning Approach

The Bharat Fellowship is built on three core learning elements:

Immersion-Based Learning

Students experience how industries operate by visiting companies, markets, and institutions across India.

CXO-Led Insights

Participants interact with business leaders who share real-world strategies, decisions, and lessons from their professional journeys.

Hands-On Business Challenges

Students apply their learning through practical assignments and group problem-solving exercises.

Programme Outcome

By the end of the fellowship, participants develop:

A deeper understanding of how businesses operate across India

Exposure to multiple industries and economic ecosystems

Practical business thinking through field-based challenges

Early insights into entrepreneurship, management, and strategy

Application Process

The admission process consists of three main stages: application submission, interview evaluation, and final admission decision.

Step 1 – Complete the Application

Applicants must fill out an online application form to be considered for the programme.

Required Information

Candidates will need to provide:

Personal details

Professional details (if applicable)

Educational background

Programme-related information

Latest resume

As part of the application, candidates must also submit an essay response.

Applicants can choose between:

Video Essay – up to 2 minutes

Written Essay – up to 200 words

This essay helps the admissions committee understand the applicant’s interests, motivations, and suitability for the fellowship.

Step 2 – Online Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited to an online interview.

Purpose of the Interview

The interview helps the admissions team:

Understand the candidate’s interests and ambitions

Assess curiosity and problem-solving ability

Discuss the programme structure and expectations

The interview panel typically includes:

Academic faculty

Industry professionals

Members of the programme’s core team

Step 3 – Final Admission Decision

After the interview, the admissions committee reviews the application and interview feedback.

The committee then makes a final decision and communicates the outcome to the applicant via email.

The entire admissions process generally takes up to four weeks from the application deadline.
