Bloomberg Equity Research Programme

The Bloomberg Equity Research Programme is a hands-on, Bloomberg-powered learning experience designed to train participants in professional financial market analysis. The programme focuses on teaching participants how to analyse financial markets, build research workflows, and extract insights from live Bloomberg data.

Participants learn how institutional investors and analysts use Bloomberg tools to conduct equity research, interpret macroeconomic signals, and monitor global financial markets.

Monthly Curriculum Structure

The programme is divided into 12 monthly modules, each focused on a specific aspect of Bloomberg-based research.

Month 1: Navigating the Bloomberg Terminal

Participants learn the core structure of the Bloomberg system.

Key topics include:

Understanding Bloomberg’s command structure

Navigating between different functions efficiently

Organising screens and saving workspaces for regular use

Month 2: Extracting Equity and Index Data

This module focuses on retrieving market data for research purposes.

Participants learn how to:

Extract historical price and volume data for Indian equities and indices

Select appropriate time periods and data frequency

Adjust data for corporate actions such as dividends and splits

Month 3: Bloomberg Excel Integration

Participants learn how to integrate Bloomberg data into Excel workflows.

Topics include:

Using the Bloomberg Excel Add-In

Structuring Excel sheets for prices and returns

Managing live data feeds versus static datasets

Month 4: Firm-Level Financial Research

This module introduces company-level financial analysis.

Participants learn how to:

Extract financial statements and ratios for listed companies

Standardise financial data across multiple firms

Build peer comparison frameworks for Indian companies

Month 5: Sector and Industry Analysis

Participants learn to analyse sector performance.

Key topics include:

Using sector classifications and indices

Comparing sector performance across economic cycles

Identifying market-leading companies using Bloomberg metrics

Month 6: Macroeconomic Market Signals

This module focuses on understanding macroeconomic factors influencing markets.

Participants learn how to:

Access Indian macroeconomic indicators

Track RBI policy decisions and economic calendars

Interpret macroeconomic trends alongside market behaviour

Month 7: Fixed Income Market Analysis

Participants explore bond markets and interest rate dynamics.

Topics include:

Accessing government bond yields and benchmarks

Reading yield curves and spread relationships

Interpreting interest rate expectations

Month 8: Cross-Asset Market Linkages

This module examines relationships between global asset classes.

Participants learn how to:

Track commodities and currency pairs

Understand transmission channels between global markets and India

Analyse cross-asset correlations

Month 9: News and Event Analysis

This module focuses on linking news flow to market behaviour.

Participants learn how to:

Track corporate announcements and macroeconomic events

Build event timelines using Bloomberg tools

Analyse price reactions to major news developments

Month 10: Event-Based Market Research

Participants conduct event-driven financial analysis.

Key topics include:

Studying price movements around important events

Defining observation windows for research

Comparing outcomes across similar events

Month 11: Market Monitoring Dashboards

Participants learn to build professional dashboards.

Topics include:

Creating dashboards to track multiple assets

Monitoring indicators across markets

Customising dashboards for daily market analysis

Month 12: ESG-Based Market Analysis

The final module explores environmental, social, and governance signals in financial markets.

Participants learn how to:

Access ESG scores and disclosures

Compare ESG metrics across companies and sectors

Interpret ESG indicators alongside financial performance

Programme Outcomes

After completing the programme, participants will be able to:

## Bloomberg Equity Research Programme

### Key Outcomes After Completion

Upon completing the **Bloomberg Equity Research Programme**, participants develop practical financial research capabilities and gain hands-on experience working with professional-grade market data tools. The programme focuses on building real analytical skills used by investors, analysts, and financial institutions.

### Bloomberg Terminal Proficiency

Participants build strong familiarity with Bloomberg’s workflow and command structure through consistent hands-on usage. By the end of the programme, they are able to navigate the platform efficiently and use it confidently for financial analysis and research tasks.

### Working with Live Market Data

Participants gain experience working with **real-time and historical multi-asset market data**, including equities, indices, bonds, commodities, and currencies. This exposure helps strengthen their research credibility and prepares them to work with the type of data used by professional trading desks and investment firms.

### Applying Bloomberg to Real Financial Analysis

The programme goes beyond basic navigation and focuses on applying Bloomberg tools to practical research use cases. Participants learn how to use Bloomberg data for company analysis, sector comparisons, macroeconomic tracking, and event-driven market research.

### Expert-Led Learning and Mentorship

Learning is guided through expert-led sessions conducted by finance professionals and Bloomberg practitioners. Participants also benefit from structured mentor interactions, which help them refine their analytical approach and understand how Bloomberg is used in real industry environments.
