UG Programme in Design
Programme Overview
1. What is the UG Programme in Design?

The UG Programme in Design is a 4-year full-time undergraduate programme designed to train the next generation of entrepreneurial designers who create innovative products, services, and ventures.

2. What is the mission of the Design programme?

The programme aims to develop entrepreneurial designers who can shape the future through innovation, creativity, and real-world problem solving.

3. What makes this design programme unique?

The programme follows a studio-first and industry-led approach, allowing students to transform bold ideas into real products, services, and ventures.

4. Who can apply for the UG Programme in Design?

The programme is open to high school graduates, making it accessible to students from diverse academic backgrounds.

5. What is the duration of the programme?

The programme duration is 4 years.

6. What is the format of the programme?

The programme follows a full-time immersive learning format.

7. Where is the programme conducted?

The programme is conducted at the Masters’ Union campus in Gurugram, located in DLF Cyberpark.

8. When does the programme start?

The next cohort of the programme commences in August 2026.

9. What kind of learning environment does the programme provide?

The programme provides a studio-based learning environment where students work on real design challenges and build practical solutions.

Programme Specializations
1. What specializations are offered in the UG Programme in Design?

The programme offers three major specializations:

Interaction Design

Industrial Design

Communication Design

2. What skills do the design specializations focus on?

The specializations combine creative craft, strategic thinking, and systems design, enabling students to create digital experiences, physical products, and visual narratives.

Interaction Design
3. What is Interaction Design?

Interaction Design focuses on designing interactive digital experiences across screens, devices, and emerging technologies.

4. What do students learn in the Interaction Design specialization?

Students learn skills such as:

User research

User experience (UX) design

Interaction design principles

Digital product design

Technical implementation for digital platforms

5. What career opportunities are available in Interaction Design?

Graduates can work in roles such as:

UX Designer

Product Designer

Interaction Designer

UI/UX Researcher

Industrial Design
6. What is Industrial Design?

Industrial Design focuses on designing physical products that improve human experiences through form, function, materials, and manufacturing processes.

7. What skills do students learn in Industrial Design?

Students learn:

Product design principles

Material selection and manufacturing

Sustainable design practices

Prototyping and fabrication technologies

8. What careers are possible with Industrial Design?

Graduates may pursue careers as:

Product Designers

Industrial Designers

Hardware Product Developers

Manufacturing Design Specialists

Communication Design
9. What is Communication Design?

Communication Design focuses on visual storytelling and brand communication using creative and strategic design approaches.

10. What skills do students learn in Communication Design?

Students learn skills such as:

Brand strategy

Creative direction

Motion design

CGI and visual design

Storytelling through design

11. What careers can students pursue after Communication Design?

Graduates may work as:

Brand Designers

Visual Designers

Motion Designers

Creative Directors

Curriculum – UG Programme in Design
Year 1 – Foundations & Broad Exposure
1. What is the focus of Year 1 in the Design programme?

Year 1 focuses on building strong foundational design skills across multiple disciplines through studio-based learning and critical thinking.

2. What skills do students develop in the first year?

Students develop skills in:

2D design

3D design

Digital media

Time-based media

Creative thinking and visual expression

3. What core courses are taught in Year 1?

Sample courses include:

Foundation Studios (Drawing, Form, Color, Space)

Design Fundamentals & Systems Thinking

Materials Lab & Fabrication Techniques

Design History, Theory & Critical Practice

4. What is the goal of the first year curriculum?

The goal is to help students build a versatile design foundation and discover their unique creative voice.

Year 2 – Applied Practice & Stream Selection
5. What is the focus of Year 2 in the Design programme?

Year 2 focuses on developing specialised design skills and applying them through interdisciplinary projects.

6. When do students choose their specialization?

Students choose their design specialization at the end of Year 2.

7. What courses are taught in Year 2?

Sample courses include:

Typography & Information Architecture

Time-Based Media

Human Factors & User Research

Computing for Designers

8. What type of projects do students work on in Year 2?

Students work on both individual and team-based design projects that integrate multiple design disciplines.

Year 3 – Advanced Specialization & Integration
9. What happens in Year 3 of the programme?

Students deepen their expertise in their chosen specialization and work on complex interdisciplinary design challenges.

10. What design specializations can students choose from?

Students can choose from:

Interaction Design (UX/UI, Service Design, AI/ML)

Industrial Design (Hard Goods, Soft Goods, Smart Products)

Communication Design (Brand Systems, Motion, Environmental Design)

11. What skills do students develop in Year 3?

Students develop skills in:

Systems-level thinking

Strategic design thinking

Research-driven design

Collaboration across disciplines

12. What type of projects do students complete in Year 3?

Students work on real-world industry challenges and complex design projects that combine strategy, research, and execution.

Year 4 – Capstone Project & Internship
13. What is the focus of the final year of the programme?

Year 4 focuses on professional readiness through a capstone thesis project and industry internship.

14. What is the capstone project?

The capstone project is a self-directed design thesis where students build an end-to-end project from research and concept development to final implementation.

15. Do students get industry experience in the final year?

Yes. Students complete a 6-month full-time industry internship at leading design studios or companies.

16. What is the goal of the final year?

The goal is to transition students from design students to professional designers ready for industry roles.
