The PG Programme in UI/UX & AI Product Design is a 16-month postgraduate programme designed to train students to become AI-first product designers. The course focuses on building skills in user experience design, interface design, product thinking, and AI-driven product workflows.

It combines design fundamentals, AI tools, and real-world product building to prepare learners for roles in modern digital product companies.

Curriculum Structure

The programme is structured across 5 academic terms, where students progressively build expertise in digital design, product thinking, AI-powered interfaces, research, and real-world product development.

Each term combines in-class learning with practical industry challenges.

Term 1: Foundations of Digital & AI-Native Design

Students learn the core language of digital products and begin integrating AI tools, collaborative systems, and code-assisted design practices.

Core Subjects
Foundations of Digital Design

Learn how to apply:

Composition

Color theory

Visual hierarchy

Layout systems

to design clear and balanced digital interfaces.

Heuristic Evaluation & Usability

Understand how to analyze digital interfaces using established usability heuristics and design principles.

Visual Storytelling for Digital Products

Learn how to convert ideas into visual narratives using:

Sketching

Storyboarding

Product storytelling frameworks

Wireframing & Interface Design

Develop UI layouts and interactive wireframes using tools like:

Figma

Framer

Typography Systems & Responsive Scaling

Design scalable typography systems that adapt across different devices and screen sizes.

AI-Driven Design Exploration

Use generative AI tools to accelerate design ideation and explore multiple visual directions.

Designing with Cursor

Learn how to collaborate with code using Cursor, enabling designers to prototype and iterate directly with code.

Term 1 Practical Challenge
Dark Pattern Audit Challenge

Goal: Increase Long-Term User Value (LTV) by 25% by replacing manipulative UI patterns with transparent design.

Students will:

Perform a Dark Pattern Audit on a popular application

Identify deceptive UX patterns such as:

Roach Motels

Hidden Costs

Forced Continuity

Redesign the product using trust-centered design principles

Run a simulated A/B test comparing deceptive UI vs ethical UI

Term 2: Research, Prototyping & Industry Practice

This term focuses on user research, information architecture, and real-world product design workflows.

Core Subjects
Research Methods I

Learn how to conduct:

Field studies

User interviews

Persona creation

Journey mapping

Information Architecture

Design scalable information structures using techniques like:

Card sorting

Content clustering

AI-assisted organization

Web Technologies for Designers

Learn the basics of:

HTML

CSS

JavaScript

to prototype and understand how digital interfaces are built.

Interface Prototyping

Build high-fidelity prototypes using:

Figma

AI plugins

Cursor for component-level coding

Concept-to-Prototype

Take a product idea from concept to working prototype through a structured design sprint.

Collaborative Design with MCP Servers

Learn real-time collaboration in shared design and coding environments.

Design Systems

Build scalable design systems using:

Design tokens

Components

UI patterns

Term 2 Practical Challenge
Ghost Cart Revenue Recovery Challenge

Goal: Increase e-commerce revenue by 15%+ by reducing cart abandonment.

Students will:

Audit and redesign a checkout flow

Build a streamlined multi-step checkout experience

Improve:

Form design

Error messaging

Value communication

Present a solution with a 3-month ROI forecast

Term 3: Design Thinking, Interaction & Intelligent Systems

This term focuses on advanced design thinking and AI-powered interactions.

Core Subjects

Term 3 Practical Challenge
AI Copilot SaaS Dashboard

Goal: Improve employee productivity by 20% by reducing time-to-insight to under 60 seconds.

Students will:

Map current data-to-decision workflows

Prototype a natural language analytics interface

Build predictive insights that suggest next best actions

Test productivity improvements during simulations

Application Timeline & Admission Process

The admission process for the PG Programme in UI/UX & AI Product Design is designed to evaluate applicants based on their creative thinking, design ability, portfolio strength, and communication skills.

The process consists of four stages.

Step 1: Complete the Application Form

Applicants must first fill out the online application form and submit the required documents.

Application Fee

₹500

Required Documents

Applicants must upload the following:

Latest resume in a clear format

Government ID for verification

Academic qualification documents

Work experience documents (if applicable) such as:

Offer letters

Internship certificates

Experience letters

Any additional certifications or achievements

These documents help the admissions team understand your academic background and professional experience.

Step 2: Submit Your Portfolio

After completing the application form and paying the application fee, applicants must submit a design portfolio.

What the Admissions Team Evaluates

The portfolio helps evaluate:

Creative thinking

Problem-solving ability

Design process

User-centered thinking

Clarity of ideas

Portfolio Requirements

Applicants may submit:

A design portfolio file

Or a portfolio website link

The portfolio can include:

UI/UX design projects

Product design case studies

Visual design work

UX research projects

Experimental design concepts

The admissions committee values clarity of thought, design process, and problem-solving ability more than visual polish alone.

Step 3: Personal Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited for a portfolio-led interview.

Interview Format

Applicants will:

Present 1–2 projects from their portfolio

Explain the problem statement

Describe their design process

Discuss key decisions and trade-offs

Interview Evaluation Criteria

The panel may ask questions related to:

Research methods used

How user flows were prioritised

Handling constraints such as:

Time

Stakeholders

Limited data

Collaboration with:

Engineers

Product managers

Decision-making and design thinking

The goal is to evaluate:

Clarity of thinking

Communication ability

Design judgment

Interview details such as date, time, and venue are shared 3–5 days in advance.

Step 4: Final Admission Decision

After reviewing the application, portfolio, and interview performance, the admissions committee makes the final decision.

Applicants fall into one of three categories.

Accepted

Applicants who receive an offer will:

Receive an admission letter

Receive the programme fee structure

Be given a timeline to confirm their seat by paying the admission fee

Waitlisted

Masters’ Union does not maintain a ranked waitlist.

Applicants in this category may receive an offer if seats become available in the cohort.

The admissions committee periodically reviews the waitlist.

Rejected

Applicants who are not selected may:

Reapply in another admission round

Apply in the next academic year with a stronger portfolio.
