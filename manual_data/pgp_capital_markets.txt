PG Programme in Capital Markets & Trading

The PGP in Capital Markets & Trading is a 12-month hybrid programme designed for individuals who want to build expertise in financial markets, trading strategies, and portfolio management. The programme combines online learning with in-person weekend classes, allowing professionals to develop advanced trading skills while continuing their careers.

This programme focuses on practical trading experience, portfolio management, and real market exposure, enabling participants to learn how financial markets operate and how to trade and invest strategically across different asset classes.

Curriculum Structure

The programme is organised into six modules, each focusing on a key area of trading and investment strategy.

Module 1: Trading Stocks Like an Expert

This module introduces participants to the fundamentals of capital markets and equity trading.

Key topics include:

Understanding how global financial markets operate

Exploring the capital markets ecosystem and its participants

Selecting the appropriate financial instruments for investment

Evaluating investment opportunities using risk-return metrics

Understanding regulatory compliance in financial markets

Learning the operational dynamics of daily market trading sessions

Participants gain the foundational knowledge required to analyse stocks and execute trades effectively.

Module 2: Using Derivatives Safely

This module focuses on derivatives trading and portfolio risk management.

Participants learn:

How to evaluate a company’s intrinsic value

The fundamentals of futures and options trading

Understanding option pricing variables such as the Greeks

Using hedging strategies including covered calls and protective puts

Protecting portfolios using derivatives

Analysing charts and technical indicators for market predictions

Implementing structured day trading strategies

This module equips participants with techniques to manage risk and maximise returns through derivatives.

Module 3: Diversifying into Commodities & Forex

The third module introduces participants to global markets beyond equities.

Topics include:

Trading commodities and understanding their pricing mechanisms

Forex trading fundamentals and currency market structures

Leveraging spot, forward, and swap markets

Applying advanced indicators to commodity and forex markets

Using strategic hedging to reduce trading losses

Analysing macroeconomic factors that influence financial markets

Applying analytical techniques such as Volume Price Analysis (VPA) and Camarilla Pivot levels

Participants learn how to diversify portfolios across multiple global asset classes.

Module 4: Portfolio Hedging & Risk Management

This module focuses on advanced financial risk management techniques used by professional investors.

Participants study:

Stress-testing portfolios using Value at Risk (VaR) and scenario analysis

Using exotic derivatives for complex hedging strategies

Managing portfolios through dynamic hedging techniques

Understanding structured financial instruments such as CDOs and ABS

Applying volatility metrics to investment decisions

Designing stochastic models for risk assessment and forecasting

These techniques help traders protect investments during volatile market conditions.

Module 5: Alternative Investments

Participants explore alternative asset classes beyond traditional financial markets.

Key topics include:

Investing in real estate through Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

Diversifying portfolios using alternative investments

Sustainable investing using ESG metrics and green bonds

Identifying investment opportunities driven by urbanisation and technology

Understanding behavioural finance and overcoming cognitive investment biases

This module helps participants build diversified investment portfolios.

Module 6: Cryptocurrency & Global Capital Flows

The final module explores digital assets and global financial systems.

Participants learn:

Fundamentals of blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies

Trading strategies for major cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum

Risk management techniques for crypto trading

Analysing global capital flows and international financial movements

Understanding taxation and regulatory aspects of global investments

The module provides insights into emerging digital financial markets.

Detailed Curriculum Structure

The PGP in Capital Markets & Trading is structured into six progressive modules, each designed to build expertise across different financial markets, trading strategies, and portfolio management techniques. The programme integrates classroom learning, practical trading exercises, financial tools training, and capstone projects to ensure participants develop both theoretical knowledge and real-world trading experience.

Module 1: Foundations of Capital Markets

This module introduces participants to the core structure of financial markets and investment analysis.

In-Class Learning

Participants begin by understanding how the global capital markets ecosystem operates.

Key topics include:

Capital Markets Ecosystem

Roles of brokers, exchanges, institutional investors, regulators, and market makers

Price discovery mechanisms including bid-ask spreads, supply-demand shifts, and market depth

Order types, volatility controls, and trading mechanics

Structure and behaviour of stock market indices

Financial Instrument Selection

Risk-return optimization using Efficient Frontier principles

Asset allocation across equities, bonds, and derivatives

Risk profiling and aligning instruments with investor objectives

Company Valuation

Reading financial statements and analysing profitability ratios

Valuation techniques such as Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) and relative valuation multiples

Interpreting quarterly earnings results

Industry benchmarking and peer comparison

Investment Risk Analysis

Risk-adjusted return metrics including Sharpe ratio, Treynor ratio, Jensen’s Alpha, and Sortino ratio

Portfolio risk estimation using Value at Risk (VaR) and Conditional VaR

Stress testing and downside risk analysis

Technical Analysis

Participants learn price prediction techniques using chart patterns and indicators:

Candlestick patterns such as Hammer, Engulfing, Doji, and Shooting Star

Support and resistance analysis

RSI divergence and volume indicators

Complex chart formations including Three Soldiers and Tower patterns

Opening Bell Trading Strategies

Pre-market analysis

Market sentiment indicators

Gap trading strategies

Scalping and volatility-based trading techniques

Out-Class Activity

Equity Hackathon with Masters’ Union Investment Fund

Participants invest and trade using ₹40 lakh real capital in the Masters’ Union investment fund. They design trading strategies, conduct equity valuations, and present risk mitigation frameworks.

Tool Training

Students learn market analysis using:

TradingView

Chartink

These platforms help identify trends, analyse charts, and track securities.

Capstone Project

Participants execute a systematic trading strategy challenge, documenting trade logic, entry and exit rules, and evaluating performance using risk-adjusted metrics.

Application Timeline & Admissions Process (Cohort 3)

The admissions process for the PGP in Capital Markets & Trading programme follows a structured multi-stage evaluation designed to assess applicants’ interest in financial markets, analytical ability, and potential to succeed in trading and investment careers.

The process consists of application submission, interview evaluation, and final admission decision.

Application Rounds
Round 1

Application Deadline: 25 February 2026

Interview Period: March 2026

Application Fee: INR 500

Status: Closed

Round 2

Application Deadline: 6 April 2026

Interview Period: March–April 2026

Application Fee: INR 500

Status: Active

Applicants must submit their application before the deadline of the respective round to be considered.

Stage 1: Complete the Application

Applicants begin the admissions process by submitting an online application form.

Required Information

The application requires candidates to provide:

Personal details

Professional details including work background and experience

This stage helps the admissions committee understand the candidate’s background and interest in capital markets and trading.

Stage 2: Online Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited for an online interview.

Purpose of the Interview

The interview allows the admissions panel to:

Evaluate the applicant’s interest in trading and financial markets

Understand their professional background and analytical skills

Assess their motivation for joining the programme

Interview Panel

The interview panel typically includes:

Academic faculty members

Industry professionals and market experts

Members of the Masters’ Union admissions team

Applicants also gain an opportunity to understand the programme structure, learning approach, and career outcomes.

Stage 3: Final Admission Decision

After the interview process, the admissions office reviews the candidate’s profile.

The final evaluation considers:

Application form and professional details

Interview performance

Overall suitability for the programme

The final decision is made by the Admissions Committee.

Applicants are informed about the result through official email communication.

Decision Timeline

The entire admissions process typically takes up to four weeks from the application deadline.
