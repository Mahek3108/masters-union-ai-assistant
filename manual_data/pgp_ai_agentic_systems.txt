## PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems

### Program Overview

The Postgraduate Programme (PGP) in Applied AI & Agentic Systems is a **15-month hands-on programme** designed to help students and professionals build practical expertise in **artificial intelligence, machine learning, and autonomous AI agents**. The programme focuses on learning through real-world projects where participants design, build, and deploy AI systems across multiple industry use cases.

Unlike traditional AI courses that focus primarily on theory, this programme emphasises **practical implementation**. Participants build **AI chatbots, autonomous agents, machine learning models, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems** while learning how these technologies can be applied in industries such as retail, healthcare, finance, and enterprise operations.

The programme combines **technical training, industry mentorship, and portfolio development**, enabling learners to graduate with deployable AI systems and a strong professional presence in the AI ecosystem.

---

## Programme Format and Duration

The programme runs for **15 months** and offers two learning formats designed to suit both fresh graduates and working professionals.

### Programme Duration

* **Duration:** 15 months
* **Cohort Start:** June 2026
* **Includes:** Live projects, industry applications, and internship opportunities

### Learning Formats

#### Offline Weekday Programme

The offline format is designed for **fresh graduates** who want a full-time immersive learning experience.

Key characteristics include:

* On-campus learning in **Gurugram** during weekdays
* Access to AI labs, studios, and collaborative workspaces
* Team-based agent building and daily collaboration sessions
* Participation in hackathons, build sprints, and coding sessions
* Internship and placement support for first-time job seekers

This format is intended for students with a **BCA, B.Tech, or computer science–related degree**, including final-year students and recent graduates.

---

#### Online Weekend Programme

The online format is designed for **working professionals** who want to transition into applied AI roles while continuing their current jobs.

Key characteristics include:

* **Live weekend classes** combined with flexible weekday assignments
* Self-paced learning modules that fit alongside a full-time job
* Opportunities to build AI solutions directly for one's workplace
* Career transition support for professionals shifting into AI-focused roles

---

## Eligibility Criteria

The programme is designed for individuals with technical backgrounds who want to develop expertise in applied AI.

Eligible applicants include:

* Candidates with **BCA or B.Tech degrees**
* Individuals with **computer science or technology-related academic backgrounds**
* **Fresh graduates** entering the AI workforce

## Project-Based Learning Approach

A defining feature of the programme is that students **build real AI systems every term**. The curriculum is structured so that each term focuses on creating an industry-grade AI solution.

### Term-Based AI Development

* **Term 1:** Develop AI tools
* **Term 2:** Apply AI solutions in industry contexts
* **Term 3:** Build advanced AI tools
* **Term 4:** Implement AI solutions in real business scenarios
* **Terms 5–6:** Develop advanced AI systems and domain applications

This progression ensures that participants gradually move from building foundational tools to designing **complex, production-ready AI systems**.

# Core AI Projects and Learning Modules

In this challenge, participants build **AI-powered conversational systems** capable of solving real-world problems. These chatbots combine programming, large language models, and automation tools to create intelligent user experiences.

Example chatbot applications include:

* A **mental wellbeing chatbot** designed to help users manage stress and anxiety using psychological principles

Participants learn essential technical skills such as:

## Curriculum Structure – PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems

The curriculum of the PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems is structured across **six academic terms (including a preparatory Term 0)**. The programme is designed to progressively develop expertise in **programming, machine learning, AI agents, RAG systems, enterprise AI deployment, and MLOps**.

Each term combines **core technical learning with real-world projects**, ensuring that participants develop practical capabilities in building deployable AI systems used in modern industries.

---

# Term 0: Foundations of Programming

The programme begins with a preparatory foundation term that focuses on **building strong programming and mathematical fundamentals** required for machine learning and AI development.

This stage ensures that all participants develop the technical base needed to work with modern AI tools and frameworks.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants learn essential concepts such as:

* Using **Python for data manipulation, cleaning, and preparation** for analytical tasks
* Understanding the **mathematical foundations of machine learning and artificial intelligence**, including probability, linear algebra, and statistical reasoning
* Writing **clean, modular, and reproducible code** that supports large AI and data science projects

These foundational skills prepare participants for the advanced AI engineering work introduced in later terms.

---

# Term 1: Data Analytics and AI Foundations

The first main academic term focuses on **data analytics, statistics, and foundational AI techniques**. Students learn how to work with structured data, extract insights, and build their first functional AI systems.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants develop capabilities in:

* Applying **statistical methods** to analyse and interpret datasets
* Using **SQL** to query, filter, and join large datasets efficiently
* Creating **data visualisations** that communicate insights clearly to decision-makers
* Building **basic AI chatbots** using prompt engineering and API-based LLM integration
* Identifying real business problems that can be solved using AI-driven approaches

### Practical Project

Participants complete the **Applied AI Chatbot Challenge**, where they design and develop an end-to-end chatbot solution. This includes identifying a real-world use case, designing prompts, integrating Python workflows with LLM APIs, and delivering a working AI system that solves a practical problem.

---

# Term 2: Agents and Automation with Modern AI

The second term focuses on **agent-based architectures and AI-driven workflow automation**. Students learn how modern AI agents interact with tools, APIs, and data sources to automate business processes.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants explore topics such as:

* Designing and implementing **modern agent architectures**
* Developing prompts, tool schemas, and system constraints to ensure reliable AI behaviour
* Integrating external tools and APIs into agent workflows
* Evaluating and testing agent performance
* Deploying and monitoring agent-based systems in production environments
* Using basic machine learning models for predictive tasks
* Understanding how automation can improve operational workflows within organisations

### Practical Project

Participants work on the **Agentic AI Accelerator for Business**, where they develop autonomous AI agents capable of completing multi-step organisational workflows. These agents are designed to retrieve data, reason over information, and execute tasks without human supervision.

---

# Term 3: Applied Machine Learning for Real Decisions

The third term introduces participants to **applied machine learning and predictive modelling**. The focus is on building ML models that support real business decisions and operational strategies.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants learn how to:

* Use **unsupervised learning techniques** to discover hidden patterns in datasets
* Build **supervised machine learning models** for classification and regression tasks
* Transform raw datasets into **ML-ready features**
* Tune machine learning models for improved accuracy while avoiding overfitting
* Deploy lightweight ML models through **API-based services**
* Communicate analytical insights effectively to business stakeholders

### Practical Project

Participants work on the **ML Consulting Garage**, where they translate business problems into machine learning solutions. Projects may involve tasks such as customer segmentation, demand forecasting, retention analysis, or pricing optimisation.

---

# Term 4: RAG Systems, Neural Networks and Data Engineering

The fourth term focuses on **building scalable AI systems powered by Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), neural networks, and robust data pipelines**.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants develop skills in:

* Using **embeddings and vector databases** for semantic search and AI applications
* Processing documents through **data ingestion, cleaning, and chunking pipelines**
* Designing and evaluating RAG architectures
* Deploying scalable RAG-based applications
* Implementing monitoring systems and feedback loops for AI models
* Understanding neural networks and pattern recognition in AI systems
* Designing **data infrastructure pipelines** capable of handling large-scale AI workloads

### Practical Project

Participants complete the **RAG System Building Challenge**, where they design and deploy a retrieval-augmented AI system capable of answering questions using private datasets while maintaining accuracy under real-world usage conditions.

---

# Term 5: Domain AI and Enterprise Machine Learning Design

This term focuses on **designing AI systems that operate within real industry constraints**, including regulatory requirements, domain-specific challenges, and enterprise-scale deployments.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants learn how to:

* Design AI solutions that respect **domain-specific operational and regulatory constraints**
* Understand how **data architecture influences AI system performance and scalability**
* Prepare for technical machine learning and AI interviews
* Build domain-focused AI solutions using supervised learning and predictive modelling

### Elective Options

Participants select one advanced elective to specialise further:

* Computer vision for real-world applications
* Reinforcement learning for decision-making systems
* Real-time AI systems using streaming data pipelines

### Practical Project

Participants take part in an **AI Immersion Programme in Retail, Healthcare, and Finance**, where they design AI solutions for real operational problems. These projects involve identifying business use cases, selecting appropriate modelling approaches, and validating solutions within industry contexts.

---

# Term 6: MLOps, AI Governance and AI Leadership

The final term focuses on **enterprise AI deployment, responsible AI practices, and leadership capabilities required to manage AI initiatives at scale**.

### Core Learning Areas

Participants develop expertise in:

* Deploying AI products using **CI/CD pipelines and monitoring frameworks**
* Building AI systems that are **reliable, ethical, and trustworthy**
* Preparing for **AI system design and behavioural interviews**
* Understanding governance frameworks for responsible AI deployment

### Elective Options

Participants can specialise in areas such as:

* AI product design and integration for product teams
* Multimodal AI systems
* Strategic AI leadership and innovation

### Practical Project

Participants continue their **industry immersion projects**, designing domain-specific AI solutions that meet business requirements while considering ethical, regulatory, and operational constraints.

---

## Overall Learning Outcome

By the end of the programme, participants graduate with a strong combination of:

* Expertise in **Python programming, machine learning, and AI system architecture**
* Hands-on experience building **AI chatbots, agent-based systems, ML models, and RAG applications**
* Knowledge of **enterprise AI deployment, MLOps, and responsible AI governance**
* A portfolio of **deployable AI systems and industry-relevant projects**

This integrated approach prepares graduates to pursue roles such as **AI engineer, machine learning engineer, AI solutions architect, AI product developer, or applied AI consultant**, while also enabling them to lead AI-driven transformation initiatives within organisations.


PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems
Enrollment Checklist & Admissions Process

The admissions process for the PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems is designed to evaluate candidates on their technical aptitude, analytical thinking, and readiness for an intensive AI programme.

The evaluation follows a holistic approach, considering academic background, technical capability, and problem-solving ability.

Admission Criteria
1. Academic Readiness

Applicants must hold a bachelor’s degree with a strong foundation in logical or quantitative thinking.

STEM backgrounds such as BCA, BTech, Computer Science, Mathematics, or Engineering are preferred.

Candidates from other disciplines may also apply if they demonstrate strong analytical and problem-solving abilities.

2. Professional Experience (If Applicable)

Applicants may have 0–5 years of experience.

Relevant experience may include roles in:

Technology

Data analytics

Product development

Software engineering

Data-driven decision making

Experience should demonstrate structured problem-solving and analytical thinking.

3. Technical Inclination

Candidates should show curiosity and comfort with technology through exposure to:

Programming languages (such as Python)

Analytics or data tools

Coding projects or technical coursework

AI, machine learning, or automation tools

4. Critical & Analytical Thinking

Applicants must demonstrate the ability to:

Break down complex problems logically

Recognize patterns and trends in data

Draw reasoned conclusions

These skills are evaluated during the technical assessment and interview stage.

Application Timeline & Process

The admission process consists of four stages.

Step 1: Online Application

Applicants begin by completing the online application form.

Required Information

Applicants must submit:

Academic transcripts (BCA / BTech / equivalent)

Professional experience proof (if applicable)

Identity details

Certifications, projects, or achievements that strengthen their profile

Video Essay Requirement

Applicants must submit a 2-minute video introduction covering:

Personal background and journey

Interest in building a career in Artificial Intelligence

Why they are a strong fit for the programme

Career goals for the next 2–3 years

Submission Guidelines

Upload the video to Google Drive

Set access to “Anyone with the link can view”

Submit the link in Part 2 of the application form

The video does not require professional editing; authenticity and clarity are preferred.

Step 2: Scholastic Technical Test

Shortlisted applicants must take the Masters’ Union Scholastic Technical Test.

This is a timed adaptive online assessment designed to evaluate analytical capability.

Test Topics

Logical reasoning

Mathematics and statistics

Programming fundamentals

The test measures the candidate’s problem-solving ability and quantitative thinking.

Step 3: Technical Interview

Candidates who pass the technical test proceed to a technical interview.

The interview is conducted by:

AI engineers

Data scientists

Faculty members

Evaluation Areas

Applicants are assessed on:

Foundational understanding of Python, data, and AI concepts

Problem-solving approach

Curiosity and learning mindset

Ability to structure and explain solutions clearly

Performance under pressure

Step 4: Admission Decision

The admissions committee evaluates each candidate holistically based on:

Application profile

Technical test performance

Interview performance

Overall fit for the programme

Final Decision Categories

Accepted
Candidates receive an offer letter and must confirm their seat by paying the admission fee within 7–21 days.

Waitlisted
Applicants may receive an offer if seats become available.

Rejected
Candidates may apply again for a future cohort.
