## PGP Rise in Owners and Promoters Management

### Curriculum Overview

The curriculum of the **PGP Rise in Owners and Promoters Management** programme is structured to help family business owners and successors master both strategic and operational aspects of running and scaling an enterprise. The programme combines **classroom learning with practical industry exposure**, enabling participants to apply concepts directly within their businesses.

The curriculum is organised into **eight modules**, each focusing on a critical dimension of leadership, governance, business growth, and wealth management.

---

# Module 1: Execution by Business Scorecard

This module focuses on converting business strategy into measurable execution frameworks.

Participants learn how to translate vision into operational goals using structured performance systems.

Key learning areas include:

* Turning strategic vision into actionable business scorecards
* Driving accountability using the **RACI framework**
* Linking rewards and incentives to performance metrics
* Aligning scorecards with both family and business objectives
* Conducting structured board reviews for stronger governance
* Integrating scorecards into daily operational management
* Connecting operational metrics with financial performance
* Using **PDCA (Plan–Do–Check–Act)** cycles for continuous improvement

### Experiential Learning

Participants attend a **Campus Residency Orientation**, where they connect with other family business leaders and industry experts.

They also participate in an **Automotive Industry Immersion**, observing how global automotive companies use precision manufacturing, robotics, and Kaizen principles to improve operational efficiency.

---

# Module 2: Digitalisation & AI

This module explores how digital technologies and artificial intelligence can transform family businesses and help them scale efficiently.

Key topics include:

* Using digital scorecards for data-driven business decisions
* Protecting organisations from cyber threats
* Improving collaboration and productivity through digital platforms
* Scaling operations using advanced technologies
* Leveraging AI to drive innovation and growth
* Enhancing customer experience using digital tools
* Building AI-enabled products and services

### Experiential Learning

Participants take part in a **Global Immersion in Europe**, studying advanced digitalisation practices and governance frameworks at institutions such as **H-Farm in Italy**.

They also interact with global family-run brands like **Ferrari and Fendi** to understand long-term growth strategies.

---

# Module 3: Dynamic People Leadership

This module focuses on leadership systems that help organisations attract, retain, and develop talent.

Participants learn how to design people strategies that align with business goals.

Key learning areas include:

* Creating organisational structures with clearly defined roles and KPIs
* Designing short-term and long-term reward systems
* Building effective performance management frameworks
* Developing strong employee value propositions
* Implementing succession planning frameworks
* Linking performance outcomes with compensation and incentives
* Developing talent pipelines to support business expansion

### Experiential Learning

Participants attend an **Executive Leadership Residency**, where family business leaders and their senior executives work together to strengthen governance, resolve leadership conflicts, and align management structures with family values.

---

# Module 4: Nurturing Family Legacy

This module addresses the unique governance challenges faced by family-owned businesses.

It focuses on building systems that ensure long-term continuity across generations.

Key topics include:

* Establishing clear rules for family members joining the business
* Designing fair wealth distribution frameworks
* Balancing tradition with digital innovation
* Defining remuneration policies and lifestyle guidelines
* Resolving conflicts through structured governance frameworks
* Integrating philanthropy and impact investing into legacy planning
* Creating family constitutions and succession plans
* Establishing advisory boards and family councils

### Experiential Learning

Participants attend a **Family Dynamics Residency**, where family members and mentors collaborate to address real governance challenges within their businesses.

---

# Module 5: Self-Mastery & Transformation

This module focuses on leadership development and personal transformation.

Participants build emotional intelligence, self-awareness, and leadership discipline.

Key topics include:

* Behavioural analysis using **Hogan and Heartstyles leadership tools**
* Practicing empathy, respect, and active listening
* Leading with stewardship and custodianship principles
* Using 360-degree feedback for leadership development
* Building psychological safety and positive organisational culture
* Developing focus, discipline, and consistency
* Leveraging personal strengths while addressing leadership gaps

### Experiential Learning

Participants receive **one-on-one coaching during their capstone project**, guided by experienced family business mentors who provide strategic insights and leadership feedback.

---

# Module 6: Product, Brand & Go-To-Market Strategy

This module teaches participants how to modernise their products and brand strategies while preserving family business heritage.

Key learning areas include:

* Designing products that balance legacy and market demand
* Crafting brand narratives that reflect family values
* Developing effective go-to-market strategies
* Redesigning pricing, packaging, and positioning for modern markets
* Professionalising marketing, sales, and digital channels
* Expanding distribution networks beyond traditional markets
* Tracking go-to-market performance using dashboards and KPIs

### Experiential Learning

Participants join an **International Industry Visit**, where they observe global manufacturing and distribution practices at major international trade events.

---

# Module 7: Strategic Wealth Management

This module focuses on building long-term financial resilience for family enterprises.

Participants learn how to manage and preserve wealth across generations.

Key topics include:

* Unlocking wealth tied up within family businesses
* Creating family offices for structured governance
* Aligning wealth management strategies with succession plans
* Preparing businesses for IPOs or strategic investors
* Diversifying investment portfolios for long-term resilience
* Preserving wealth across generations
* Integrating tax planning and risk management into financial strategy

### Experiential Learning

Participants take part in an **F&B Industry Immersion**, studying successful family-run brands such as **Haldiram’s and Varun Beverages**, and learning how they combine tradition with innovation to achieve large-scale growth.

---

# Module 8: CXO & Business Owner Insights

The final module provides strategic insights from senior executives and experienced business owners.

Participants learn how large organisations build governance systems, scale operations, and manage complex leadership structures.

Key topics include:

* Aligning CXO contributions with board-level strategy
* Scaling businesses through structured systems and processes
* Building strong brand equity and long-term reputation
* Transforming risks into large business opportunities
* Building teams that emphasise integrity and accountability
* Learning profit strategies and talent systems from large conglomerates
* Managing large-scale projects for sustainable growth

### Experiential Learning

The programme concludes with a **Campus Residency Convocation**, where participants join a strong network of industry leaders, mentors, and fellow business owners, creating long-term partnerships for collaboration and growth.

Programme Process & Admissions Overview

The PGP Rise in Owners and Promoters Management programme is designed as a structured partnership between participants and experienced industry mentors. The process combines strategic assessment, customised learning, hands-on implementation, and long-term advisory support to help family businesses scale sustainably.

Step-by-Step Guide: How the Programme Works

The programme follows a structured approach that helps participants diagnose business challenges, implement strategic solutions, and sustain long-term growth.

1. Business Needs Assessment

The first stage focuses on understanding the participant’s business environment, challenges, and growth objectives.

This step helps the programme team tailor the learning experience to the specific needs of each family business.

Key activities include:

Identifying core business challenges and operational gaps

Defining clear business and leadership objectives

Evaluating current operational strengths and organisational capabilities

This assessment ensures that the programme addresses real business problems rather than generic theoretical scenarios.

2. Custom Curriculum Design

Based on the business assessment, the programme team designs a customised learning journey.

The curriculum is adapted to reflect the participant’s:

Business structure

Industry challenges

Family governance dynamics

Strategic growth ambitions

Key elements of this stage include:

Development of tailored learning modules

Focus on family business leadership and governance

Use of practical case studies relevant to real business situations

This ensures the curriculum directly supports practical business transformation.

3. Strategic Implementation Support

The programme emphasises real-world implementation rather than theoretical learning.

Participants receive structured guidance while applying strategies within their businesses.

Key support includes:

Developing actionable strategic plans

Working on hands-on business projects

Receiving continuous feedback from mentors and advisors

This stage helps participants convert classroom insights into measurable operational improvements.

4. Ongoing Board Impact Support

Support continues even after the core learning phase.

Participants receive ongoing advisory assistance to strengthen governance, leadership, and long-term growth.

This includes:

Regular advisory board sessions

Access to an expert network of industry leaders

Continuous strategic guidance for sustained business development

This long-term engagement ensures that the programme delivers lasting organisational impact.

Admission Process

The admissions process consists of three stages.

Step 1: Complete the Online Application

Applicants must submit an online application form to be considered for the programme.

Required information includes:

Personal details

Professional details

Applicants may optionally submit a Statement of Purpose (SOP) of up to 500 words, explaining their motivation for joining the programme and their business goals.

Step 2: Online Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited for an online interview.

The interview allows the admissions panel to:

Understand the applicant’s business background

Assess leadership potential and growth ambitions

Provide clarity about the programme structure and expectations

The interview panel typically includes:

Academic faculty members

Industry experts

Members of the programme team

Step 3: Final Admission Decision

After the interview, the admissions office evaluates the complete application.

The final decision is made by the Admissions Committee, which reviews:

Application details

Professional background

Interview performance

Programme fit

Applicants are notified of the final decision via email communication.

The entire admission process typically takes up to four weeks after the application deadline.
