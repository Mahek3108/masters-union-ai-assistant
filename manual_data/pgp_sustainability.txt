PGP in Sustainability & Business Management

The Post Graduate Programme in Sustainability & Business Management is a 12-month full-time programme designed for graduates who want to build careers in the intersection of business strategy, climate action, and sustainability leadership.

The programme focuses on preparing professionals to work in ESG strategy, carbon management, sustainability consulting, and climate-tech businesses.

PGP in Sustainability & Business Management
Programme Curriculum Overview

The PG Programme in Sustainability & Business Management is structured across 8 academic terms, designed to progressively build expertise in sustainability strategy, ESG frameworks, climate finance, renewable energy, circular economy, and sustainable supply chains.

The curriculum combines theoretical learning, industry case studies, and practical challenges that simulate real-world sustainability problems faced by businesses.

Term 1: Fundamentals of Business & Sustainability

The first term introduces the foundations of sustainability and how it intersects with business strategy, finance, and market positioning.

Students explore how global environmental challenges are reshaping corporate strategy and decision-making.

Global Sustainability Landscape

Students examine international sustainability frameworks and global policy movements including:

The Paris Climate Agreement and COP climate conferences

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Comparative sustainability strategies across major economies such as the EU, United States, China, and India

This helps students understand the global regulatory and environmental context influencing modern businesses.

Integrating Sustainability into Business Strategy

Students learn how organisations incorporate sustainability into operational and strategic planning.

Key areas include:

Ecosystem mapping across suppliers, customers, and government bodies

Identifying operational inefficiencies such as waste and emissions

Materiality assessment to prioritise sustainability initiatives

Identifying high-impact intervention points across value chains

Global Sustainability Best Practices

The programme analyses sustainability strategies adopted by leading companies including:

Unilever

IKEA

Tesla

Google

H&M

Students evaluate how sustainability can become a competitive advantage rather than merely a compliance requirement.

Business Finance Fundamentals

Students develop financial literacy required for sustainability decision-making.

Key concepts include:

Interpreting financial statements such as Profit & Loss, Balance Sheets, and Cash Flow statements

Evaluating investment decisions using metrics such as NPV, IRR, and ROI

Understanding cost structures and contribution margins

Managing cash flow and operational runway

Sustainable Brand Strategy

Students explore how sustainable products are positioned and marketed.

Topics include:

Consumer sustainability behaviour and purchasing patterns

Pricing strategies for sustainable products

Building brand trust and avoiding greenwashing

Messaging and positioning strategies for environmentally responsible brands

Practical Challenge

Students participate in a Sustainable Dropshipping Challenge, where they launch a sustainability-focused online business. This includes:

Validating product-market fit

Auditing supplier sustainability claims

Building an online store

Running marketing campaigns

Managing fulfilment and measuring sustainability impact

Term 2: Governance, Policy & ESG

The second term focuses on regulatory frameworks, governance systems, and ESG reporting standards that guide corporate sustainability practices.

ESG Reporting and Compliance

Students learn how companies produce credible sustainability disclosures.

Key frameworks studied include:

Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)

SEBI’s Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR)

International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB)

IFRS sustainability standards

Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)

Students also study stakeholder mapping, materiality assessments, and methods to identify greenwashing risks.

Government Policies and Regulations

Students explore how environmental regulations influence corporate operations in India.

Key topics include:

The Environment Protection Act (1986)

ESG compliance frameworks under SEBI

Industry case studies including companies such as HUL, ITC, Tata Motors, and Maruti Suzuki

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

Students study how organisations leverage CSR initiatives to build brand credibility and community engagement.

Topics include:

CSR compliance and reporting

Employee participation in CSR initiatives

Community partnership models

Measuring social and environmental impact

Leadership Communication

Students develop leadership communication skills including:

Public speaking

Persuasive communication

Business writing

Negotiation techniques

Executive-level presentations

Practical Challenge

Students participate in The Policy Playbook Challenge, where they design a sustainability policy or regulation addressing sector-level environmental challenges.

Term 3: Carbon Markets & Climate Finance

This term focuses on the financial mechanisms driving climate action and the economic systems built around carbon reduction.

Carbon Markets

Students learn how carbon trading systems function.

Key topics include:

Compliance vs voluntary carbon markets

Carbon credit creation and lifecycle

Carbon pricing dynamics

Verification and integrity checks

Climate Technology Ecosystem

Students study emerging climate technology companies and investment models.

Examples include companies such as:

Sun Mobility

ReNew Power

Ather Energy

Students also examine infrastructure and regulatory requirements enabling climate technology growth.

Climate Finance

Students explore financial instruments supporting sustainability investments, including:

Green bonds

Sustainability-linked loans

Renewable energy project financing

Carbon Accounting

Students learn how companies measure and manage emissions using the GHG Protocol.

Topics include:

Scope 1 emissions (direct emissions)

Scope 2 emissions (energy-related emissions)

Scope 3 emissions (supply chain emissions)

Climate Investment Analysis

Students analyse how venture capital and private equity investors evaluate climate-tech businesses.

Examples include funds such as:

Breakthrough Energy Ventures

TPG Rise

Temasek

BlackRock Climate Funds

Practical Challenge

Students complete a Sustainable Investment Challenge, designing a corporate sustainability roadmap aligned with a 2030 net-zero strategy.

Renewable Energy Ecosystem

Industry Leaders

Practical Challenge

Students complete the Green Supply Chain Rearchitecture Challenge, redesigning supply chains to achieve significant carbon emission reductions.

Term 7: Sustainability Sector Labs

Students choose a specific industry and study its sustainability challenges in depth.

Available sectors include:

Electric mobility and transport

Construction and real estate

Textiles and apparel

Pharmaceuticals and healthcare

FMCG and consumer goods

Students analyse industry dynamics, environmental impact, and sustainability strategies within these sectors.

Industry Internship

Students complete a professional internship where they work on real sustainability initiatives such as:

ESG reporting

Decarbonisation strategy

Supply chain optimisation

Term 8: Careers, Consulting & Ventures

## Fee Structure – PGP in Sustainability & Business Management

The **PG Programme in Sustainability & Business Management** follows a structured payment schedule across three stages of the admission and programme timeline. The total programme fee is **INR 22,65,000**.

### Payment Schedule

**1. Admission Offer Acceptance**
To confirm the admission seat, students must pay an **admission fee of INR 75,000** within **30 days of receiving the admission offer**. This payment secures the candidate’s place in the programme cohort.

**2. Programme Commencement Fee**
Before the start of classes, students are required to pay the **first instalment of the tuition fee amounting to INR 10,95,000**. This payment enables students to begin the programme and access academic resources, classes, and programme activities.

**3. Mid-Programme Instalment**
After approximately **three months into the programme**, students must pay the **second tuition instalment of INR 10,95,000**. This instalment covers the remaining academic period of the programme.

### Total Programme Cost

The overall programme fee structure is summarized as follows:

* Admission Fee: **INR 75,000**
* First Tuition Instalment: **INR 10,95,000**
* Second Tuition Instalment: **INR 10,95,000**

**Total Programme Fee: INR 22,65,000**

This fee covers the academic curriculum, learning resources, and programme-related activities conducted during the **12-month postgraduate programme**.
