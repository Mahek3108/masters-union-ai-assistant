## PGP in Sports Management & Gaming

### Program Overview

The Postgraduate Programme (PGP) in Sports Management & Gaming is a **16-month full-time postgraduate program** designed for individuals who want to build careers at the intersection of **sports, gaming, media, analytics, and business strategy**.

The programme blends **core business management education with specialised training in sports management and the rapidly growing gaming ecosystem**. It focuses on preparing students to work across areas such as **sports leagues, esports platforms, athlete management, fan engagement, sports media, analytics, and entrepreneurship**.

Built around Masters’ Union’s **“learning by doing” philosophy**, the programme encourages students to gain practical experience through projects, competitions, and startup building rather than relying solely on traditional classroom learning.

The programme is delivered **on campus with an optional residential component**, enabling students to engage with faculty, industry leaders, and peers in an immersive learning environment.

---

## Program Format and Duration

The PGP in Sports Management & Gaming is structured as a **full-time, on-campus programme lasting 16 months**. It integrates classroom instruction with industry exposure and experiential learning opportunities.

Key programme details include:

* **Programme Duration:** 16 months
* **Programme Format:** Full-time, on-campus (optional residential facility available)
* **Commencement Date:** June 2026
* **Admission Deadline (Round 3):** 15 March 2026

Throughout the programme, students participate in **live industry projects, internships, and domestic or international immersion opportunities**, enabling them to gain real-world experience in the sports and gaming industries.

---

## Eligibility Criteria

The programme is open to candidates from diverse academic backgrounds who are interested in building careers in sports management, gaming, or related industries.

Eligible applicants include:

* Individuals holding a **Bachelor’s degree in any discipline**
* **Fresh graduates** with up to **11 months of experience**
* **Early career professionals** with **1–5 years of work experience**

This flexible eligibility structure allows students from both academic and professional backgrounds to transition into the sports and gaming industry.

---

## Learning Experience and Faculty

The programme emphasises **industry-integrated learning**, ensuring that students gain insights directly from professionals actively working in the sports and gaming sectors.

Students learn from **full-time faculty members, sports and gaming entrepreneurs, CXOs from sports organisations, and former professional athletes**. This combination of academic expertise and industry leadership ensures that learning remains practical and relevant.

Key elements of the learning experience include:

* **Live case studies with CXOs from leading sports platforms**, allowing students to analyse real strategic decisions and industry challenges
* **Direct mentorship from former athletes and gaming pioneers**, providing unique insights into the operational and competitive aspects of sports organisations
* **Industry-led workshops and immersive projects**, designed to simulate real business scenarios in sports leagues, gaming platforms, and media organisations

This approach helps students develop both **business acumen and sector-specific expertise**.

---

## Curriculum Structure

The programme curriculum is organised across **eight academic terms**, gradually moving from general business knowledge to specialised sports and gaming expertise and finally to real-world professional engagement.

### Terms 1–2: Core Business Foundations

The first two terms focus on building **fundamental business knowledge**, including strategy, finance, marketing, and management. Students study these core subjects alongside peers from the **PGP in Technology & Business Management cohort**, ensuring a strong foundation in general management.

These terms help students understand how businesses operate before applying those principles to the sports and gaming industries.

---

### Terms 3–5: Specialised Sports and Gaming Courses

In the middle phase of the programme, students begin exploring specialised topics related to sports and gaming ecosystems. Coursework may cover areas such as:

* Sports management and league operations
* Esports and gaming ecosystems
* Sports media and fan engagement strategies
* Sports analytics and performance data
* AI and analytics applications in sports organisations

These terms focus on building domain expertise required to manage teams, events, platforms, and fan communities.

---

### Terms 6–8: Immersions, Live Projects, and Internships

The final stage of the programme focuses on **practical industry exposure**.

Students participate in:

* **Live industry projects with sports organisations or gaming companies**
* **Professional internships** that provide real workplace experience
* **Domestic or international immersions** that expose students to global sports ecosystems

This phase ensures that students graduate with hands-on industry experience in addition to academic knowledge.

---

## Startup and Entrepreneurship Opportunities

A distinctive feature of the programme is its strong focus on **entrepreneurship within the sports and gaming industries**. Students are encouraged to build and test startup ideas throughout the programme.

Examples of ventures students may explore include:

* Sports tournaments and league management platforms
* Fantasy sports leagues
* Gaming communities and esports teams
* Sports analytics platforms
* Athlete branding and fan engagement platforms

The programme emphasises **learning business by building real ventures**, giving students the opportunity to experiment with business models and generate revenue.

### Startup Learning Journey

Students participate in several entrepreneurial challenges during the programme:

* **Term 1 – Dropshipping Challenge**
  Students build and operate an e-commerce venture to understand sales, marketing, and operational management.

* **Term 2 – Fantasy League and Campus Competitions**
  Students organise fantasy leagues and sports competitions, gaining experience in event management and fan engagement.

* **Term 3 onwards – Startup Development**
  Students begin working on their own sports or gaming startup ideas, testing business models and building market traction.

## Industry Relevance and Career Context

The global sports industry is valued at **over $600 billion**, and the gaming industry continues to expand rapidly with increasing digital engagement worldwide.

In India alone, there are **more than 400 million gamers and sports fans**, and the sector is growing at approximately **15% annually**. This growth is creating new opportunities across areas such as:

* Professional sports leagues
* Fantasy sports platforms
* Esports organisations
* Sports media and content creation
* Data analytics and performance technology
* Athlete branding and digital fan engagement

These industries are increasingly driven by **technology, analytics, and digital innovation**, making them attractive fields for business professionals.

## Curriculum Structure – PGP in Sports Management & Gaming

The curriculum of the PGP in Sports Management & Gaming is structured across **eight terms**, combining **business fundamentals, specialised sports and gaming knowledge, hands-on projects, and industry exposure**. The programme emphasises experiential learning, ensuring that students apply theoretical knowledge through challenges, tournaments, immersive visits, and live industry projects.

---

# Term 1: Business Foundations

The first term focuses on building **core business competencies** that are essential for leadership roles in sports and gaming organisations. Students learn how business functions such as finance, marketing, technology, and communication operate and how they contribute to building successful sports ventures and gaming platforms.

### Core Learning Areas

Students develop foundational knowledge across multiple disciplines:

* **Communication and Self-Development**
  Learning how to deliver compelling presentations and communicate ideas effectively through inspiring speeches and leadership communication.

* **Finance and Fintech**
  Understanding how to read and analyse financial statements to evaluate business performance.

* **Sales and Marketing**
  Learning cost-effective marketing strategies and how businesses can advertise and grow without large budgets.

* **Management and Strategy**
  Understanding teamwork, leadership dynamics, and how to collaborate effectively in organisational environments.

* **Product and Technology**
  Learning how digital platforms are built by creating and managing an e-commerce website.

* **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning**
  Understanding prompt engineering and how generative AI can be used to improve productivity and decision-making.

* **Liberal Arts and Global Context**
  Studying global political and economic shifts, including how world events influence industries and markets.

### Experiential Learning

Students participate in hands-on activities such as:

* **Dropshipping Challenge**
  Students launch their own e-commerce websites, run marketing campaigns, manage supply chains, and compete to generate revenue.

* **Domestic Industry Immersion**
  Students visit sports organisations, gaming studios, corporate offices, and stadiums to gain real-world exposure to the industry.

---

# Term 2: Strategy and Growth

The second term focuses on developing **strategic thinking, analytics skills, and operational decision-making capabilities**. Students learn how organisations grow and how data, product strategy, and operations influence business performance.

### Core Learning Areas

Key learning areas include:

* **Personal Finance and Self Development**
  Understanding financial planning and responsible money management.

* **Finance and Business Information Systems**
  Learning how to design and implement Management Information Systems (MIS) to track and evaluate business performance.

* **Marketing Optimisation and Revenue Growth**
  Understanding how to improve conversion rates and increase average order value through data-driven marketing strategies.

* **Economic Systems and Market Behaviour**
  Exploring how national economies function and how economic systems impact business growth.

* **Product Thinking and Innovation**
  Learning how to adopt a product mindset that focuses on solving real user problems.

* **Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models**
  Understanding how AI systems and large language models operate and how they are transforming industries.

* **Global Economic Growth**
  Examining how countries generate wealth and sustain economic development.

### Experiential Learning

Students participate in several practical projects:

* **Pre-Seed Cheque Challenge**
  Students develop startup ideas and compete for over ₹15 lakh in pre-seed funding by building and pitching their Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

* **Domestic Industry Immersion**
  Students travel to cities such as Jalandhar to study manufacturing and supply chain operations at companies like Nivia, while also exploring broadcasting and commercial rights operations in Mumbai.

* **Sports Tournament Management**
  Students organise and run their own sports tournaments, such as pickleball or padel competitions in Delhi-NCR, gaining hands-on experience in event operations and fan engagement.

---

# Term 3: The Business of Sport

This term introduces students to the **global sports industry and the operational frameworks of leagues, teams, events, and athlete brands**. Students learn how sports businesses function and how fan engagement and sponsorship strategies drive revenue.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Global Sports Ecosystems**
  Understanding how international sports leagues, federations, and teams operate.

* **League and Team Management**
  Learning the operational and strategic requirements for running professional sports teams and leagues.

* **Sports Marketing and Sponsorships**
  Exploring how brands partner with sports organisations and measure the impact of sponsorship deals.

* **Athlete Brand Management**
  Understanding how athletes build personal brands and manage their public image.

* **Venue and Event Operations**
  Learning how large sporting events are organised, including logistics, budgeting, safety, and fan experience management.

### Experiential Learning

* **VIP–MVP Product Development Challenge**
  Students convert their classroom learning into a test-ready product or platform for the sports or gaming market.

* **Sports Trek**
  Students participate in behind-the-scenes visits to leagues, teams, and gaming studios to observe operations firsthand.

* **Sports Streaming Challenge**
  Students explore the economics of sports broadcasting and create their own sports streaming channel.

---

# Term 4: Gaming, Media & Technology

The fourth term focuses on the **rapidly evolving intersection of gaming, esports, digital media, and sports technology**. Students learn how technology is transforming sports entertainment, fan engagement, and athlete performance.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Gaming Economics and Monetisation**
  Understanding revenue models for games including in-app purchases, advertising, and subscriptions.

* **Esports Operations and Community Building**
  Learning how esports teams, tournaments, and gaming communities are managed and scaled.

* **Digital Media and Sports Broadcasting**
  Exploring how streaming platforms, broadcasting rights, and production networks shape the sports media industry.

* **Fan Engagement and Content Strategy**
  Designing digital content strategies that attract, engage, and monetise sports fans.

* **Sports Technology and Performance Analytics**
  Using sensors, AI, and data analytics to improve athlete performance and team strategy.

### Experiential Learning

* **VIP – Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy Project**
  Students design a market entry strategy for sports or gaming products and platforms.

* **International Immersion**
  Students travel abroad to explore global sports and gaming ecosystems through industry visits, workshops, and live league experiences.

---

# Term 5: Strategy, Analytics & Leadership

The fifth term prepares students to make **high-level strategic decisions within sports organisations and gaming businesses**. Students learn how finance, analytics, legal frameworks, and leadership principles influence the sports ecosystem.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Sports Finance and Revenue Models**
  Understanding financial structures including team valuations, revenue streams, salaries, and transfer fees.

* **Data-Driven Decision-Making in Sport**
  Using analytics dashboards, metrics, and predictive models to support strategic decisions.

* **Sports Law, Ethics and Governance**
  Understanding contracts, regulations, athlete rights, and league governance structures.

* **Future of Sports and Gaming**
  Exploring how emerging technologies such as AI, augmented reality, virtual reality, and wearables will transform the industry.

* **Leadership in High-Performance Teams**
  Learning leadership strategies for managing athletes, creators, and sports organisations under high-pressure conditions.

### Experiential Learning

* **VIP – Product Market Fit Challenge**
  Students refine their sports or gaming product ideas and develop strategies to improve customer acquisition and engagement.

* **Domestic Industry Immersion**
  Students travel to major sports cities in India to observe how leagues, clubs, and sports organisations operate in practice.

---

# Terms 6–8: Live Industry Projects and Internship

The final stage of the programme focuses on **real-world professional experience** through industry-backed projects and internships.

Students work directly with **sports franchises, sports marketing agencies, gaming studios, and sports foundations**, applying their learning to real operational and strategic challenges.

### Industry Engagement Activities

* **Consulting or Entrepreneurial Project**
  Students collaborate with sports organisations or gaming companies to solve real business challenges.

* **Operational Industry Experience**
  Students apply classroom learning in areas such as sponsorship management, sports analytics, event operations, or digital product development.

* **Mentorship from Faculty and Industry Experts**
  Students receive guidance from experienced professionals throughout the project period.

* **Raising-the-Seed-Fund Challenge**
  Students pitch startup ideas to venture capital investors in a format similar to a “Shark Tank” presentation to raise funding in exchange for equity.

---

### Overall Learning Outcome

By the end of the programme, students graduate with a combination of:

* Strong **business management foundations**
* Deep understanding of the **sports and gaming ecosystem**
* Experience in **sports marketing, analytics, and event management**
* Skills in **digital media, esports, and sports technology**
* Real-world experience through **industry projects, tournaments, and startup development**

This combination of **business education, practical projects, and industry exposure** prepares graduates to pursue careers in sports leagues, esports organisations, media platforms, gaming startups, and sports technology companies.
