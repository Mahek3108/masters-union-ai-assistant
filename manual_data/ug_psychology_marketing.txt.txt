UG Programme in Psychology & Marketing
1. What is the UG Programme in Psychology & Marketing?

The UG Programme in Psychology & Marketing is a 3+1 dual degree programme with Illinois Institute of Technology (Illinois Tech) that combines behavioural psychology with practical marketing skills.

2. What does the 3+1 dual degree structure mean?

The programme follows a 3+1 structure, where students study:

3 years at Masters’ Union in Gurugram

1 year at Illinois Institute of Technology (Chicago, USA)

Students receive global academic exposure and international learning experience.

3. What makes this programme unique?

The programme integrates behavioural psychology with hands-on marketing, allowing students to understand how consumers think and how marketing strategies influence behaviour.

4. What skills do students develop in this programme?

Students develop skills in:

Consumer psychology

Marketing strategy

Branding and communication

Behavioural analysis

Data-driven marketing decisions

5. Does the programme focus on practical learning?

Yes. The programme emphasises learning by doing, where students apply psychology concepts to real-world marketing scenarios and campaigns.

6. How does behavioural psychology relate to marketing in this programme?

Students learn how human behaviour, emotions, and decision-making processes influence consumer choices, which helps in creating effective marketing strategies.

7. What global exposure does the programme provide?

Students gain international exposure through the dual degree structure, including studying at Illinois Tech in Chicago.

8. What career paths can this programme lead to?

Graduates can pursue careers in:

Marketing strategy

Brand management

Consumer behaviour analysis

Digital marketing

Advertising and communications

Curriculum – Psychology & Marketing Programme
Year 1 – Foundations of Mind & Market
1. What do students learn in Year 1 of the Psychology & Marketing programme?

In Year 1, students build a strong foundation in psychology and marketing fundamentals, learning how human behaviour influences business decisions and marketing strategies.

2. What psychology subjects are taught in the first year?

Students study several psychology-focused courses, including:

Introduction to Psychology

Neuroscience

Developmental Psychology

Social Psychology

Cognitive Psychology

Environmental Psychology

Stress Management

These courses help students understand how people think, behave, and make decisions.

3. Do students learn about personality and behaviour?

Yes. The People and Personalities course helps students understand personality traits and how they influence work, communication, and decision-making.

4. Do students learn research methods in psychology?

Yes. The Psychological Research course teaches students how to analyse human behaviour and uncover hidden consumer insights.

5. What marketing subjects are introduced in Year 1?

Students learn marketing fundamentals through courses such as:

Building Marketing Strategies

Consumer Behaviour

Scaling a D2C Business

6. Do students learn communication skills?

Yes. The Art of Communication course teaches students how to deliver clear and inspiring presentations.

Experiential Learning in Year 1
7. Do students build their own business in Year 1?

Yes. Students participate in a Dropshipping project, where they build a D2C brand from scratch and aim to generate ₹10 lakh or more in revenue.

8. Does the programme include global exposure in Year 1?

Yes. Students can participate in a Global Immersion Programme, traveling to countries such as:

United States

Italy

Singapore

France

to learn about global marketing strategies and consumer behaviour.

9. What is the "Hack Your Consumer Mindset" project?

In this project, students collaborate with a local restaurant to redesign its menu and customer experience, aiming to increase sales by 20% using behavioural insights.
