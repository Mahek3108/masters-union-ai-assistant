Entrepreneurship & Business Acceleration Program (EBAP)

The Entrepreneurship & Business Acceleration Program (EBAP) is a specialised programme designed to help Indian business owners significantly increase sales, improve profitability, and automate core operations. It focuses on practical strategies that entrepreneurs and family businesses can implement immediately within their organisations.

The programme emphasises real-world implementation rather than theory, enabling participants to apply proven business practices that drive measurable growth.

Curriculum Overview

The curriculum focuses on five major areas that directly impact business growth and operational efficiency.

Module 1: Increase Sales

This module focuses on practical strategies to grow revenue in the Indian market.

Participants learn how to:

Use WhatsApp and Instagram marketing effectively for Indian customers

Design incentive structures for sales teams without relying on discount wars

Expand into Tier-2 and Tier-3 markets

Shift from informal selling approaches to value-based selling strategies

Upsell to existing customers who already trust the brand

Partner with distributors, kirana stores, and e-commerce platforms to expand reach

Build strong product branding through better packaging and positioning

Create simple sales dashboards to track sales performance efficiently

Module 2: Maximise Profits

This module focuses on improving margins and strengthening financial control.

Participants learn how to:

Access financing from banks, NBFCs, and angel investors

Negotiate better deals with suppliers and manage procurement efficiently

Calculate the true cost of products, including hidden operational expenses

Adopt digital payment systems to reduce cash leakages

Improve customer payment collections and reduce delays

Use government schemes, subsidies, and MSME benefits to support business growth

Module 3: Prevent Business Leakages

This module addresses operational inefficiencies that reduce profitability.

Participants learn how to:

Set up a simple CRM system using tools like Google Sheets

Use CCTV and business software such as Tally, Zoho, and Vyapar to prevent theft or pilferage

Map internal processes to identify areas where time and money are being wasted

Apply practical lean management principles suited to Indian businesses

Digitally track inventory rather than relying on manual systems

Module 4: Business Automation

This module focuses on creating systems that allow businesses to scale without constant owner involvement.

Participants learn how to:

Outsource non-core activities such as logistics, accounting, and HR

Hire reliable managers while protecting business relationships

Create Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) to streamline operations

Design clear organisational structures and reporting systems

Use AI tools such as ChatGPT, Zapier, and Indian automation tools

Professionalise hiring practices beyond informal recruitment networks

Structure executive leadership roles such as hiring a CEO

Build simple MIS dashboards to monitor business performance

Create decision frameworks that reduce dependence on the owner

Move gradually from operator → owner → investor

Module 5: Family Harmony

Family businesses often face governance and relationship challenges. This module focuses on managing these dynamics effectively.

Participants learn how to:

Create wills and succession plans to avoid legal disputes

Develop a family constitution defining roles, compensation, and governance rules

Train the next generation before transferring leadership responsibilities

Separate family matters from business decision-making

Establish fair compensation structures for family members

Use independent advisors or mentors to resolve conflicts

Manage ego conflicts between family members through clear role definitions

Establish family councils for structured decision-making

Balance traditional family values with professional business practices

Build businesses that sustain across multiple generations

Entrepreneurship & Business Acceleration Program (EBAP)
Execution Framework, Admissions Process, and Fee Structure

The Entrepreneurship & Business Acceleration Program (EBAP) is designed around a structured three-stage execution framework that ensures participants not only learn business strategies but also implement them within their own companies. The programme focuses on diagnosing real business problems, building practical solutions, and delivering measurable results.

Three-Stage Execution Framework

The EBAP programme transforms theory into practice through a structured implementation process that guides participants from identifying business challenges to executing solutions and measuring impact.

Stage 1: Diagnose Core Challenges

The first stage focuses on identifying the real obstacles preventing business growth.

Participants begin by selecting one critical business challenge affecting their organisation. This may include areas such as:

Sales optimisation

Delegation and operational systems

Profitability improvement

Team and people management

Through structured diagnostic tools, participants analyse the root causes behind these challenges.

Key activities include:

Conducting Balanced Scorecard-based diagnostics

Founder interviews and business assessments

Reviewing operational systems such as dashboards, SOPs, and reporting structures

Analysing how the founder’s time is allocated within the organisation

This stage helps participants uncover operational and strategic bottlenecks and define measurable goals that can realistically be achieved within 60–90 days.

Stage 2: Build Tactical Solutions

Once the core business problem is identified, the programme guides participants in designing practical solutions.

Participants create a customised execution playbook tailored to their business needs.

This stage includes:

Implementing structured scorecards and performance frameworks

Deploying operational tools such as ClickUp for task and project management

Creating Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Building cash flow management systems

Developing job descriptions and organisational structures

Designing performance incentives and reward systems

Participants combine insights from all programme modules to build a comprehensive implementation strategy that integrates both strategic and operational improvements.

They also develop:

A business tracking dashboard

Weekly review systems

Team alignment strategies

Support mechanisms from mentors and industry CXOs

This stage ensures that solutions are not only well-designed but also practically executable.

Stage 3: Implement and Validate

The final stage focuses on executing the transformation plan inside the participant’s organisation.

Participants implement the designed solutions with continuous guidance from programme faculty, mentors, and industry experts.

Key elements include:

Executing operational improvements within their business

Addressing real-time challenges through one-on-one coaching sessions

Participating in founder clinics and peer feedback discussions

Iterating solutions based on feedback and results

The objective is to deliver measurable improvements such as:

Increased lead generation

Improved cash collections

Reduced dependency on the founder for daily operations

Improved team productivity and accountability

Participants present their transformation journey and business results during the final residency through a capstone presentation, demonstrating quantified improvements achieved during the programme.

Application Journey

The admissions process for EBAP is designed to evaluate applicants based on their business experience and growth ambitions.

Step 1: Online Application

Applicants must submit an online application to be considered for the programme.

The application requires:

Personal details

Professional background

Information about the applicant’s business

Applicants are typically expected to have at least 2–3 years of business experience.

Step 2: Online Interview

Shortlisted applicants are invited for an online interview.

This conversation allows the admissions team to understand:

The applicant’s business journey

Their growth ambitions

The challenges they want to solve through the programme

The interview also provides applicants with an opportunity to interact with faculty members and learn more about the programme structure.

Step 3: Final Admission Decision

The Admissions Committee reviews the application and interview feedback to make the final decision.

Applicants are notified of the result through email.

The entire process typically takes around four weeks from the application deadline.
