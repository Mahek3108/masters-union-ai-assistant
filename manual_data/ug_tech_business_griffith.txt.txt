UG Programme in Technology & Business Management (Griffith University – Australia)
Programme Overview
1. What is the UG Programme in Technology & Business Management with Griffith University?

The UG Programme in Technology & Business Management is a 4-year dual degree programme offered in partnership with Griffith University, Australia, combining business education with international exposure.

2. What is the structure of the Griffith University dual degree programme?

The programme follows a 2+2 structure:

First 2 years: Study at Masters’ Union in Gurugram, India

Final 2 years: Study at Griffith University in Australia

3. What is the format of the programme?

The programme is offered in a full-time format with on-campus learning.

4. Where do students study during the programme?

Students study across two campuses:

Masters’ Union, Gurugram (India)

Griffith University, Australia

5. When does the programme begin?

The programme commences in August 2026.

6. What is the Round 3 application deadline?

The Round 3 application deadline is 13th May 2026.

7. Who is eligible to apply for this programme?

Students who are:

Currently in Class XII, or

Pass-outs from 2024 onwards

are eligible to apply.

8. What is the total duration of the programme?

The programme duration is 4 years.

9. How many years do students study in India and Australia?

Students study:

2 years in India (Masters’ Union)

2 years in Australia (Griffith University)

10. What benefits do students gain from the international study experience?

Students gain:

Global academic exposure

International business education

Access to global career opportunities and networks

UG Programme in Technology & Business Management (Griffith University)
Year 1 – Foundations of Technology & Business
1. What do students learn in the first year of the Technology & Business Management programme?

In the first year, students learn the core foundations of business and technology, including accounting, economics, problem solving, and data visualisation.

2. Which core courses are taught in Year 1?

Core subjects include:

Art of Communication – Learning to deliver inspiring presentations and speeches

Introduction to PowerPoint & Excel – Building comfort with business presentation tools

Legalities in Business – Understanding how law affects business decisions

Fundamentals of Accounting – Reading and analysing financial statements

Macroeconomics – Understanding how national economies work

Microeconomics – Studying market behavior and economic decision making

Problem Solving and Structuring – Learning structured approaches to solving business problems

Managerial Accounting – Analysing unit economics and financial performance

Introduction to Product Management – Developing a product mindset

Building Marketing Strategies – Creating go-to-market (GTM) strategies using funnels

Fundamentals of Statistics – Using statistical methods for business insights

Mastering Data Visualisation – Using tools like Tableau and SQL for business analytics

3. What practical experiences do students gain in Year 1?

Students participate in several hands-on learning experiences, including:

Dropshipping Challenge
Students build their own D2C brand and aim to generate ₹10 lakh or more in revenue.

Global Immersion Programme
Students travel to international innovation hubs such as Japan, Singapore, Germany, and Silicon Valley.

Shopfloor Internship
Students gain hands-on operational experience by working directly in manufacturing or operational environments.

Year 2 – New-Age Business Skills
4. What skills do students develop in the second year?

In the second year, students develop modern business and technology skills, including product analytics, venture capital, digital marketing, and corporate finance.

5. What subjects are taught in Year 2?

Key courses include:

Auditing & Ethics – Learning how independent financial audits are conducted

Managerial Behaviour – Developing leadership and management skills

Supply Chain & Logistics – Understanding global procurement and shipping systems

Introduction to HRM – Learning how to work effectively in teams

Corporate Strategies – Building long-term business strategies

Corporate Governance – Understanding governance and ethical decision making

Corporate Finance – Managing budgets and financial planning

Venture Capital & Fintech – Understanding startup funding and fintech systems

Design Thinking & Prototyping – Applying design thinking to product development

Product Analytics – Using analytics to improve product performance

Digital Marketing – Running digital campaigns on platforms like Meta and Google

Consumer Behaviour – Using psychology to position brands effectively

Introduction to Machine Learning – Understanding the fundamentals behind AI systems

6. What real-world experiences do students gain in Year 2?

Students participate in several applied projects, including:

Content Creation Challenge
Students grow a digital presence on platforms like YouTube or Instagram, aiming to reach 5,000+ followers while learning content strategy.

Corporate Internship
Students intern with companies to gain real-world exposure to leadership, communication, and stakeholder management.

Global Immersion Programme
Students visit international locations to study global businesses, culture, and social systems.

Year 3 & 4 – Global Study in Australia

UG Programme in Technology & Business Management (Griffith University – Australia)
Lateral Entry
1. Does Masters’ Union offer lateral entry for the Technology & Business Management programme?

Yes. Masters’ Union offers a Lateral Entry pathway that allows students studying in the first year at another university to transfer into the Undergraduate/IPM Programme in Technology & Business Management.

2. Who is eligible for lateral entry?

Students who are currently enrolled in the first year of another university programme can apply for lateral entry.

3. What benefits do students get after transferring through lateral entry?

Students gain access to:

Industry-aligned curriculum

Mentorship from industry experts

Hands-on projects and real-world learning experiences

Global exposure through international study opportunities

4. Is the admission process different for lateral entry applicants?

No. The online admission process is the same for all applicants, including lateral entry candidates.
