PGP Bharat – An Immersion-Driven Business Programme

PGP Bharat is a unique management programme designed around experiential learning across India. Instead of relying only on classroom teaching, the programme takes participants on a 7,000-km journey across 20+ Indian cities, allowing them to learn business directly from factories, markets, enterprises, and industry leaders.

Participants gain real exposure by working on consulting projects, internships, industrial visits, and CXO interactions while travelling across different economic hubs of the country.

Programme Overview
Format

Travel-based programme with a hybrid learning structure:

Business travel immersions across India

Hybrid weekend classes for structured learning

Duration

24 weeks

The programme consists of:

8 weeks of travel-based immersions

16 weeks of hybrid weekend classes

Immersions

Participants experience 40+ industry immersions, including consulting assignments, internships, and industrial visits.

Commencement Date

May 2026

Admissions are currently open for the May 2026 cohort.

Cohort	Start Date	Application Status	Admission Status
Cohort 2	May 2026	Active	Round 2 in progress (approximately 80% of seats filled)
The PGP Bharat Journey

The programme offers a 7,000-kilometre learning journey across 20+ cities, combining business travel, CXO sessions, and industry immersion experiences.

Participants learn how businesses operate across different parts of India—from traditional markets to modern industrial ecosystems.

The journey includes exposure to:

Traditional retail markets

Manufacturing hubs

Startup ecosystems

Corporate headquarters

Government institutions

Key Learning Hubs

The travel journey is organised into multiple regional hubs across India.

Hub 1: Delhi NCR

Participants begin their journey in Delhi, where they explore both historic and modern business ecosystems.

Learning experiences include:

Visiting Chandni Chowk to understand India’s traditional retail networks

Exploring fast-growing D2C brands

Exposure to institutions such as Parliament, the Election Commission of India (ECI), and the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)

Attending 9 CXO sessions with industry leaders

Hub 2: Jalandhar

Jalandhar represents India’s manufacturing ecosystem.

Participants learn about:

Export-oriented manufacturing businesses

MSME-led industrial growth

Regional supply chains and global exports

Hub 3: Mundra & Ahmedabad

This hub exposes participants to large-scale infrastructure and industrial operations.

Learning areas include:

Logistics and port operations

Industrial supply chains

Corporate operations within large conglomerates

Hub 4: Mumbai

Mumbai provides exposure to India’s financial and corporate ecosystem.

Participants learn about:

Corporate leadership and large-scale enterprises

Financial markets and investment ecosystems

Startup and venture capital networks

Hub 5: Goa

Goa offers insights into tourism-driven business ecosystems and hospitality entrepreneurship.

Participants observe:

Tourism industry operations

Hospitality management

Lifestyle-driven business models

Hub 6: Bengaluru

Bengaluru represents India’s startup and technology hub.

Participants explore:

Startup ecosystems

Technology innovation

Venture capital networks and product development

Hub 7: Darjeeling

Darjeeling introduces participants to regional industries such as tea production and rural enterprise models.

Participants study:

Agricultural supply chains

Export-driven tea industry operations

Rural economic systems

Hub 8: Lucknow

Lucknow exposes participants to regional trade, manufacturing clusters, and traditional business ecosystems.

Participants gain insights into:

Regional markets

Small and medium enterprise ecosystems

Local supply chain networks

The Three Learning Pillars of PGP Bharat

The programme is built around three core learning pillars: Experience, Exposure, and Extraction.

Experience – Learn by Travelling

Participants learn directly from real business environments across India.

Key elements include:

25+ business immersions

10+ cultural immersions

Travel across 20+ cities

A 7,000-km learning journey

This approach enables participants to understand how businesses operate in different regional and cultural contexts.

Exposure – Learn by Collaborating

Participants interact directly with business leaders, industry experts, and corporate executives.

Key exposure opportunities include:

Sessions with 40+ CXOs and mentors

Interactions with Fortune 500 companies and leading unicorn startups

Insights from millions of real Indian consumers and markets

This helps participants understand how large organisations and emerging businesses operate.

Extract – Learn by Building

Participants convert learning into practical outcomes by building projects, ventures, and business experiments.

Hands-on learning includes:

Developing business skills through Venture Initiation Programme (VIP) and dropshipping experiments

Creating a personal digital presence through the Creator Challenge

Building investment or business portfolios through 10+ hands-on assignments

This ensures participants leave the programme with tangible outputs and real business experience.

Fee Structure – PGP Bharat Programme

The PGP Bharat programme follows a straightforward fee structure covering admission and the full learning experience, including tuition, travel, and immersive industry visits.

Programme Fee Breakdown
Rounds	Non-Refundable Admission Fee	Tuition + Travel + Immersions	Total Programme Fee
Round 1	INR 50,000	INR 17,50,000	INR 18,00,000
Round 2	INR 50,000	INR 17,50,000	INR 18,00,000
Fee Components Explained

Admission Fee (Non-Refundable)
A one-time fee of INR 50,000 paid after receiving the admission offer to confirm participation in the programme.

Tuition + Travel + Immersions
This component includes:

Academic instruction and faculty-led sessions

Travel across 20+ cities in India

Industry immersions and company visits

Consulting assignments and internships

Cultural experiences and learning residencies

Total Programme Cost

The total fee for the programme is INR 18,00,000, covering the entire learning journey including classroom sessions, travel experiences, and industry immersions across India.

This integrated fee structure ensures that participants receive both academic learning and real-world exposure as part of a single comprehensive programme.
