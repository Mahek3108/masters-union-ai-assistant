## PGP in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy (HR & OS)

### Program Overview

The Postgraduate Programme (PGP) in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy is a **16-month full-time postgraduate program** designed to develop the next generation of HR leaders who can operate as strategic partners within modern organisations. The programme integrates **core business management education with specialised learning in human resources, organisational culture, and people strategy**.

Rather than focusing only on traditional HR processes, the programme prepares students to understand **how organisations grow, how teams function, and how people, technology, and data collectively influence business performance**. It aims to produce HR professionals who can design organisational structures, build strong cultures, and drive long-term strategic outcomes for companies.

The programme is delivered **on campus with an optional residential component**, allowing students to engage in immersive learning experiences while collaborating closely with peers, faculty, and industry experts.

---

### Program Format and Duration

The PGP in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy is structured as a **full-time, on-campus programme lasting 16 months**. The programme is designed to provide a balance between academic learning, real-world exposure, and professional development.

Key program details include:

* **Programme Duration:** 16 months
* **Programme Format:** Full-time, on-campus (optional residential facility available)
* **Commencement Date:** June 2026
* **Admission Deadline (Round 3):** 15 March 2026

In addition to classroom learning, the programme includes **live industry projects, internships, and optional domestic and international immersion opportunities**, enabling students to gain practical exposure to real organisational challenges.

---

### Eligibility Criteria

The programme is open to candidates from diverse academic backgrounds who are interested in pursuing a career in human resources and organisational strategy.

Eligible applicants include:

* Individuals holding a **Bachelor’s degree in any discipline**
* **Fresh graduates** with up to **11 months of experience**
* **Early career professionals** with **1–5 years of work experience**

This broad eligibility ensures that both recent graduates and professionals early in their careers can benefit from the programme’s strategic HR training.

---

### Learning Experience and Faculty

The programme places a strong emphasis on **industry-integrated learning**, combining academic instruction with real-world exposure from experienced practitioners.

Students learn from a mix of **full-time faculty, Chief Human Resource Officers (CHROs), and senior HR leaders** who have extensive experience in building organisational cultures and aligning people strategy with business goals.

Key elements of the learning experience include:

* **Live business projects with partner companies**, allowing students to apply HR frameworks to real organisational challenges
* **Direct mentorship from industry leaders**, providing guidance on strategic HR leadership and career development
* **Industry-led workshops and immersive projects**, focused on practical HR problem-solving and organisational design

This blended learning model ensures that students gain both **conceptual understanding and practical expertise**.

---

### Curriculum Structure

The programme curriculum is designed to develop HR professionals who can operate as **business strategists rather than process managers**. The academic structure is divided into **eight terms**, each progressively building expertise in business fundamentals, HR strategy, and real-world consulting.

**Terms 1–2: Core Business Foundations**
The initial terms focus on building a strong foundation in core business disciplines such as management principles, business strategy, organisational behaviour, and decision-making frameworks.

**Terms 3–5: Specialisation in Human Resources and Organisation Strategy**
During these terms, students focus on specialised HR topics such as talent strategy, organisational design, culture building, leadership development, and people analytics.

**Terms 6–8: Live Projects and Consulting Practice**
The final stage of the programme emphasises applied learning through live consulting projects. Students work with companies to analyse organisational challenges, design HR strategies, and implement practical solutions.

This structure ensures that students graduate with **both strategic understanding and practical HR consulting experience**.

---

### Internship Experience

As part of the programme, students participate in a **three-month internship with leading companies**, including startups, technology firms, and established businesses.

The internship experience allows students to work on real organisational initiatives and gain hands-on exposure to HR leadership practices. During this period, students may work on projects such as:

* Designing **modern organisational structures and talent strategies**
* Applying **people analytics to support data-driven HR decision-making**
* Developing **leadership development and organisational capability programs**

This industry exposure helps students bridge the gap between classroom learning and professional practice.

---

### Industry Relevance and Career Context

The modern workplace is undergoing rapid transformation due to **advances in artificial intelligence, remote and hybrid work environments, and global talent mobility**. As organisations adapt to these changes, the role of HR has evolved significantly.

Human resources is no longer limited to administrative processes. Instead, HR leaders are increasingly responsible for shaping **organisational culture, talent strategy, leadership development, and workforce analytics**.

Despite India producing approximately **500,000 MBA graduates each year**, fewer than **5% specialise in HR**, creating a significant gap in the supply of skilled HR professionals. At the global level, the **Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)** has become one of the **most in-demand C-suite roles**, second only to the Chief Technology Officer (CTO).

---

### Program Objective

The PGP in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy aims to address this gap by developing **future-ready HR leaders** equipped with expertise in:

* Business fundamentals and organisational strategy
* People analytics and data-driven decision-making
* Organisational design and culture development
* Leadership and talent management

Through a combination of **academic learning, industry mentorship, internships, and live consulting projects**, the programme prepares graduates to contribute meaningfully to organisations and help shape the future of work.


## Curriculum Structure – PGP in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy

The curriculum of the PGP in Human Resources & Organisation Strategy is structured across **eight terms**, designed to progressively build expertise in **business foundations, strategic HR capabilities, organisational leadership, and real-world industry practice**.

The programme combines **classroom learning, practical projects, industry exposure, and consulting assignments**, ensuring that students develop both conceptual knowledge and applied HR leadership skills.

---

# Term 1: Business Foundations

The first term focuses on building **core business knowledge** required for HR professionals to operate as strategic partners within organisations. Students develop a strong understanding of finance, marketing, management, communication, and technology while also learning how these functions interact with human capital strategy.

### Core Learning Areas

Students are introduced to key foundational concepts through practical, application-focused modules:

* **Communication and Self-Development**
  Learning how to deliver compelling presentations and inspire teams through effective communication and leadership presence.

* **Finance and Fintech**
  Understanding how to read and analyse financial statements to interpret business performance and support strategic decision-making.

* **Sales and Marketing**
  Exploring cost-efficient marketing strategies and understanding how businesses create demand and customer engagement.

* **Management and Strategy**
  Learning how to collaborate effectively within teams and manage cross-functional work environments.

* **Product and Technology**
  Developing practical knowledge of digital products, including how to set up and manage an e-commerce website.

* **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning**
  Understanding prompt engineering and learning how to leverage generative AI tools for productivity and decision-making.

* **Liberal Arts and Global Context**
  Studying global political and economic shifts, including how world events influence business and organisational environments.

### Practical Application

During this term, students participate in the **Dropshipping Challenge**, where they build and operate their own e-commerce websites. This experience exposes students to marketing campaigns, supply chain management, and revenue generation, providing a practical understanding of how businesses operate.

---

# Term 2: Strategy and Growth

The second term expands students’ understanding of **business strategy, analytics, and operational decision-making**. Students learn how organisations scale, how economic systems influence businesses, and how technology and analytics support strategic growth.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Communication and Personal Finance**
  Developing financial literacy and learning how to manage personal and professional financial decisions.

* **Finance and Business Analytics**
  Understanding how to create Management Information Systems (MIS) that help organisations track performance and make data-driven decisions.

* **Sales and Marketing Optimisation**
  Learning how to improve conversion rates and increase average order value through data-driven marketing strategies.

* **Management and Economic Systems**
  Understanding how national economies function and how macroeconomic conditions influence businesses.

* **Product Thinking and Innovation**
  Developing a product mindset that focuses on solving customer problems and creating scalable solutions.

* **Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models**
  Exploring how AI systems and large language models work and how they are transforming industries.

* **Global Economic Development**
  Examining how countries grow economically and generate wealth through policy, innovation, and trade.

### Practical Application

Students participate in multiple experiential learning initiatives:

* **Pre-Seed Cheque Challenge**
  Students develop startup ideas, validate business concepts, and pitch them for potential pre-seed funding. The objective is to build and demonstrate a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

* **Live Industry Immersion**
  Students visit the headquarters of companies and startups to observe how HR functions operate within real organisations.

---

# Term 3: The Business of People

The third term marks the transition from general business education to **specialised HR and organisational strategy training**. This term focuses on understanding how organisations attract, develop, and retain talent while using analytics and technology to drive workforce decisions.

This term is **developed and taught in collaboration with Aon**, providing industry-driven expertise in HR practices.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Strategic Talent Acquisition and Employer Branding**
  Learning how organisations design hiring strategies and build strong employer brands to attract high-quality talent.

* **Learning and Capability Development**
  Designing training systems that build future-ready skills and support organisational capability development.

* **Total Rewards and Compensation Strategy**
  Understanding how to structure compensation, incentives, and benefits to improve employee performance and retention.

* **People Analytics and Workforce Insights**
  Using data analytics to support workforce planning and strategic HR decision-making.

* **HR Technology and Generative AI**
  Leveraging HR technology platforms and generative AI tools to improve HR efficiency and decision-making.

* **Talent Management and Retention**
  Identifying high-potential employees, building leadership pipelines, and implementing retention strategies.

### Practical Application

Students participate in several applied HR projects:

* **VIP–MVP Execution Project**
  Transforming theoretical HR frameworks into actionable organisational strategies.

* **Talent Acquisition Sprint**
  Managing real hiring processes including candidate sourcing, assessment design, interviews, and hiring decisions.

* **HR Capstone Project**
  Solving a real organisational challenge by designing an end-to-end people strategy.

---

# Term 4: People Strategy and Change Leadership

This term focuses on developing the leadership capabilities required to **drive organisational transformation and manage large-scale change initiatives**.

Students learn how HR leaders partner with business executives to align people strategy with long-term organisational growth.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Incentive Design and Performance Rewards**
  Designing short-term and long-term incentive systems aligned with organisational performance goals.

* **HR Business Partnering and Change Management**
  Learning how HR professionals collaborate with leadership teams to implement strategic transformation initiatives.

* **Organisational Psychology and Leadership Behaviour**
  Applying behavioural science to improve leadership effectiveness and team dynamics.

* **HR Across Organisational Stages**
  Understanding how HR practices differ between startups, growth-stage companies, and large enterprises.

* **Organisation Design and Culture Scaling**
  Designing organisational structures and cultures that can adapt as companies grow.

### Practical Application

Students work on projects such as:

* **Go-To-Market Strategy Project (VIP–GTM)**
  Developing strategies that help organisations successfully launch new products or initiatives.

* **Employer Branding Strategy Project**
  Designing employer branding campaigns, recruitment content, and talent acquisition funnels.

---

# Term 5: Strategic HR Leadership and the Future of Work

The fifth term prepares students to operate as **strategic HR leaders in rapidly evolving workplaces shaped by technology, automation, and new workforce models**.

### Core Learning Areas

* **Future of Work**
  Exploring how artificial intelligence, automation, and digital transformation will reshape organisational structures and workforce strategies.

* **HR Leadership in the Boardroom**
  Learning how HR leaders influence high-level strategic decisions by providing insights about talent and organisational capability.

* **Legal, Ethics, and Diversity in the Workplace**
  Understanding regulatory frameworks, workplace ethics, and diversity, equity, and inclusion practices.

* **HR Consulting and Transformation**
  Learning how to diagnose organisational problems, redesign HR processes, and implement large-scale transformation initiatives.

### Practical Application

Students participate in multiple experiential projects:

* **VIP–Product Market Fit Challenge**
  Developing strategies to improve product adoption, customer engagement, and business sustainability.

* **Culture Audit Lab**
  Conducting organisational culture assessments using interviews, analytics, and employee feedback data.

* **Domestic or International Immersion (Optional)**
  Students may participate in immersion programs that provide exposure to different industries and global business practices.

---

# Terms 6–8: Live Industry Projects and Professional Certification

The final phase of the programme is dedicated to **practical industry engagement and advanced professional development**. Students work directly with companies and industry leaders to design and implement real HR initiatives.

### Industry Projects and Practical Work

* **People Strategy Capstone Project**
  Students collaborate with companies to design comprehensive workforce strategies addressing real organisational challenges.

* **HR Consulting Lab**
  Advisory projects conducted with senior HR leaders and CHROs to develop practical consulting skills.

* **Culture Audit and Organisational Reflection**
  Evaluating company cultures and proposing transformation strategies based on data-driven insights.

### Professional Certification

Students also receive training aligned with **International Coaching Federation (ICF) standards**, equipping them with leadership coaching and mentorship capabilities.

---

### Overall Learning Outcome

By the end of the programme, students graduate with a strong combination of:

* **Business management knowledge**
* **Strategic HR expertise**
* **People analytics and HR technology skills**
* **Organisational design and leadership capabilities**
* **Real-world consulting and project experience**

This integrated learning approach prepares graduates to become **strategic HR leaders capable of shaping modern organisations and driving the future of work**.
