UG Programme in Data Science & AI (Global – Illinois Tech)
Programme Overview
1. What is the UG Programme in Data Science & AI?

The UG Programme in Data Science & AI is a 4-year undergraduate programme focused on building real-world AI products and data-driven solutions through hands-on learning.

2. What is the teaching approach of the programme?

The programme follows a learn-by-building approach, where students create AI tools, applications, and data-driven systems while studying core data science and AI concepts.

3. What is the format of the programme?

The programme is offered in a full-time format with on-campus learning.

4. Where is the programme conducted?

The programme is conducted across two campuses:

Masters’ Union, Gurugram (India)

Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago (USA)

5. What is the 3+1 programme structure?

The programme follows a 3+1 structure:

First 3 years: Study at Masters’ Union in Gurugram

Final year: Study at Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago

6. When does the programme begin?

The programme commences in August 2026.

7. What is the Round 3 application deadline?

The Round 3 application deadline is 13th May 2026.

8. Who is eligible to apply for the programme?

Students who:

Have completed or are currently in Class XII

Have Mathematics as a subject

are eligible to apply.

9. What is the duration of the programme?

The programme duration is 4 years.

10. What degree do students receive after completing the programme?

Students graduate with a Bachelor’s degree in Data Science & Artificial Intelligence, including international academic exposure.

11. Do students study abroad during the programme?

Yes. Students spend one year studying at Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago.

12. What global benefits do students get from the programme?

Students gain:

International academic exposure

Access to global research and AI ecosystems

Networking opportunities with international companies

1. Who teaches in the Data Science & AI programme?

The programme is taught by AI engineers, product leaders, and industry experts from companies such as PwC, Ola Krutrim, Microsoft, Google, PayPal, and American Express.

2. How many industry experts are involved in the programme?

The programme includes 100+ industry experts who contribute to teaching, mentorship, and workshops.

3. What kind of mentorship do students receive?

Students receive mentorship from:

30+ CTOs and product leaders

Professors from Illinois Tech and leading institutions

Experts from organisations such as Google, MIT, and NASA

4. What is the mentor-to-student ratio in the programme?

The programme offers a 1:5 mentor-to-student ratio, allowing personalised learning and guidance.

Curriculum & Learning Approach
5. How is the curriculum designed for the programme?

The curriculum is application-first and industry-built, developed in collaboration with PwC and Ola Krutrim.

6. What type of practical learning facilities are available?

Students get access to:

A 5000 sq ft prototyping lab

Field projects with 50+ organisations

Opportunities to build four real products during the programme

7. What is the “learn by shipping” approach?

Students build and launch real AI products each term, solving real-world industry problems rather than focusing only on theoretical learning.

8. How many AI products do students build before graduation?

Students build and launch 40+ AI products during the programme.

9. Do student-built products reach real users?

Yes. Student-built applications have reached 10,000+ real users.

10. Do students present their projects to industry leaders?

Yes. Students demonstrate their products to 100+ venture capitalists, CXOs, and technology leaders.

Internships
11. When do internships begin in the programme?

Students can start internships from the first year of college.

12. Which companies offer internships to students?

Students have interned with companies such as:

Microsoft

Razorpay

Ola Krutrim

13. What percentage of students secure internships in the first year?

Approximately 75% of students secure internships within their first year.

14. Do students get international internship opportunities?

Yes. Students have worked across:

India

UAE

United States

Singapore

Global Exposure
15. Does the programme offer international study opportunities?

Yes. Students spend their fourth year studying at Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago.

16. What degree do students receive after completing the programme?

Students graduate with a Bachelor of Science (B.S.) in Artificial Intelligence from Illinois Tech (USA).

17. What global opportunities do students gain from this programme?

Students gain:

Access to Illinois Tech’s global internship network

Exposure to international AI ecosystems

Networking with alumni across three continents

Admissions – UG Programme in Data Science & AI (Global)
Application Process
1. What is the admission process for the Data Science & AI programme?

The admission process consists of four main stages:

Complete the Application Online

Submit the Video Essay

Submit Test Scores (JEE / SAT / MUSAT)

Admission Decision

Step 1 – Online Application
2. What documents are required for the application?

Applicants must submit:

Class X marksheet

Class XI marksheet

Class XII passing certificate (for students graduating in 2024, 2025, or 2026)

3. Are test scores required for admission?

Yes. Applicants must submit one of the following:

JEE (Session 1 or Session 2) scores

SAT scores

If students do not have these scores, they must take the Masters’ Union Scholarship Aptitude Test (MUSAT).

Step 2 – Video Essay
4. Is a video essay required for the application?

Yes. Applicants must submit a 2-minute video essay within 7 days of submitting the online application.

5. What should students include in the video essay?

Students should discuss:

Their interest in technology or AI

Any projects, applications, or internships they have worked on

Personal achievements or experiences related to technology

6. What are the technical requirements for the video essay?

The video must:

Be maximum 2 minutes long

Be uploaded to Google Drive

Have sharing enabled as “Anyone with the link can view”

Step 3 – Test Scores
7. What exams are accepted for admission evaluation?

Applicants can submit scores from:

JEE

SAT

If they do not have these scores, they must take the Masters’ Union Scholarship Aptitude Test (MUSAT).

8. How does the MUSAT test work?

Students taking MUSAT will:

Receive a 7-day window to complete the test

Get only one attempt at the main test

Receive a practice test before the main exam

Step 4 – Admission Decision
9. What admission outcomes are possible?

Applicants may receive one of the following decisions:

Accepted

Rejected

Waitlisted

10. What happens if a student is accepted?

Accepted students receive:

Admission confirmation

Fee structure for the programme

Students must confirm admission by paying the admission fee within 7–21 days.

11. What happens if a student is waitlisted?

Waitlisted applicants may receive admission if additional seats become available.

12. Can rejected applicants apply again?

Yes. Rejected applicants can apply again in the next academic year.

Lateral Entry
13. Does the programme allow lateral entry?

Yes. Students studying in the first year at another university can apply for lateral entry into the programme.

14. Is the admission process different for lateral entry?

No. The online admission process remains the same for all applicants.

Fee Structure
15. What is the total tuition fee for the Data Science & AI global programme?

The total tuition fee is approximately ₹1,14,80,000, excluding additional personal expenses.

16. Is there an admission fee?

Yes. Students must pay a non-refundable admission fee of ₹1,80,000.

17. What are the tuition fees for each year?
Year	Tuition Fee
Year 1	₹10,80,000
Year 2	₹15,95,000
Year 3	₹17,85,000
Year 4 (Illinois Tech)	₹70,20,000
18. Why is the fourth-year tuition higher?

The fourth year is completed at Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago, which includes international tuition fees and academic resources.

19. How is the fourth-year tuition calculated?

The fourth-year fee is approximately $78,000 converted to INR using an exchange rate of 1:90.
The final cost may vary depending on the exchange rate at the time of study.

Hostel Fees
20. Are hostel accommodations available?

Yes. Hostel accommodation is available in Gurugram for the first three years of the programme.

21. What are the hostel accommodation options?

Twin Sharing: ₹27,500 per month

Single Sharing: ₹48,500 per month

22. Does the hostel fee include electricity and taxes?

No. Electricity charges and applicable taxes are not included in the hostel fee.
