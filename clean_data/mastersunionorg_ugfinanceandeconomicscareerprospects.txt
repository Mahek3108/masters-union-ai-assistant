approach to investment decisions.
Both the Boardroom Simulation and interview will take place on
the same day, across major cities in India, with Zoom options

the core content requirements for professional certifications like Chartered Accountant (CA)
and Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA). This allows students to simultaneously prepare for
these certifications alongside their degree, giving them a significant head start on their
professional qualification journey.
Is it mandatory to choose a certification (CA or CFA) or sit for the
No, the decision to sit for the external
professional exams (CA or CFA) is entirely optional. The programme is designed to provide the
curriculum and preparation necessary for these pathways. However, the choice to pursue the
official certification, including registering for and taking the external exams, remains
with the student. Your Bachelor's degree from Masters' Union is a stand-alone qualification.

Is switching from CA to CFA allowed?
Yes, in principle, it is allowed, but the process has two parts:
Internal Academic Flexibility (Masters' Union): If a student decides in the
first year to switch their focus, then you will not lose the credits from
your academic degree. However, we allow case by case basis in the first
External Certification (ICAI/CFA Institute): This is the main point of
clarity: you cannot switch credit between the two certification bodies. CA
(ICAI) and CFA (CFA Institute) are independent, external qualifications.
If you pay the registration fee and clear a level of one (e.g., CA
Foundation) and then switch to the other (CFA), you will have to start from
Level I of the new certification. The fees paid for the first certification
are non-transferable and non-refundable to the new certification.
Is switching from CFA to CA allowed?
No, this is not allowed given CA has a lot
of statutory components and a mandatory articleship.

What is the proportion of Finance vs Economics in the course?
The UG Programme in Finance & Economics
with CA/CFA Pathways is primarily weighted towards Finance (Applied), with Economics
(Foundational) serving as a critical supporting pillar. The programme uses a strong foundation
in Economics to provide context for a deep dive into Applied Finance, all while being
infused with Data and Technology skills necessary for the modern financial professional.
Since the curriculum is aligned to CA/CFA qualifications, The percentage of Economics in the
curriculum is the same as its weightage in the CA/CFA professional syllabus.

What is a boardroom simulation? How to prepare for it?
The in-person boardroom simulation will
require students to role-play as a CXO of a company and solve its business challenges in a
team of 5-6 applicants. We will send them a case study to prepare for the boardroom
simulation round 3-4 days prior for preparation. In this round, they will be judged on their
creativity, communication, and teamwork skills. They will be participating in a group
discussion for 45 minutes followed by a presentation (without any slides).
